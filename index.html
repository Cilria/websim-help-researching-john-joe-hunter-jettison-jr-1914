What if the Umayyad Caliphate had successfully conquered India in the 8th century?

I've been thinking about this scenario lately. We know the Umayyads made it as far as Sindh in 712 CE under Muhammad bin Qasim, but what if they had actually conquered the entire Indian subcontinent? How would this have changed Indian history, culture, language, and religion? Would we see a complete Islamization like in Persia, or would it be more like Spain with significant Christian/Muslim interaction? What happens to Hinduism and Buddhism in this timeline?

The linguistic implications are enormous. Arabic would likely replace Sanskrit as the prestige language, similar to how it replaced Coptic in Egypt. However, India's linguistic diversity might resist complete Arabization. We'd probably see Arabic heavily influencing local languages, creating Islamic versions of Hindi, Bengali, Tamil, etc. Think of it like Urdu but on steroids - massive Arabic vocabulary, Arabic script for all languages, and Arabic as the language of education and government. Sanskrit might survive as a liturgical language for Hindu minorities, similar to Latin in medieval Europe.
