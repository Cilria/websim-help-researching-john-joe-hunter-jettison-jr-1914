Genealogy Research on James Monroe Crowder (1843-1929) - Need help finding more details on his Civil War service and late-life marriage

Posted by u/GenealogyHunter92 • 3 hours ago • 47 upvotes • 23 comments

Hi everyone! I've been researching my 3rd great-grandfather James Monroe Crowder and have hit some interesting roadblocks. Here's what I've confirmed so far:

Born: April 10th, 1843 in Crawford, MS
Died: July 20th, 1929 in Brooksville, MS
Parents: Edmund Crowder Sr. (1801-1865) and Martha Ann Wilks (1805-after 1870)

He was their 8th child and never attended school - started working on the farm at age 8. Four of his brothers enlisted in the CSA in November 1861, and James joined in March 1862 at age 19.

According to his pension file, he served from March 1862 to December 1864, reenlisted January 1865 until the end of the war. He was never captured by Union forces. In July 1864, he was shot through the shoulder and broke his left shoulder when he fell.

After the war, he moved to a farm east of Brooksville and worked there the rest of his life. He married Eliza and had 10 children, but here's where it gets weird - in 1924, at age 81, he married a 22-year-old woman named Susan!

I'm looking for any additional records about his Civil War service (which regiment he was with specifically), and more details about this late-life marriage to Susan. Has anyone encountered similar age-gap marriages in their research? The family lore suggests he was quite poor, so I'm wondering what the circumstances were.

Also, his pension file mentions brothers Nathanial and David died during the war, while Robert and Edmund Jr. survived. I'd love to find their service records too if anyone has tips on Mississippi CSA records.

89 points CivilWarBuff • 3 hours ago
Found him! James M. Crowder enlisted March 15, 1862 in Company K, 40th Mississippi Infantry. His service record shows he was wounded at the Battle of Atlanta on July 22, 1864 - that matches his pension file about being shot in the shoulder. He was hospitalized in Macon, Georgia and returned to duty in October 1864.

Okay, this is fascinating - I found the marriage record for James and Susan. Susan's full name was Susan Mae Thompson, born 1902. Found something interesting about his post-war poverty. The 1880 agricultural census shows James owned 40 acres but had only $150 in farm equipment and $50 in livestock. By comparison, his neighbor William Thompson (Susan's grandfather) had 200 acres and $800 in equipment. The Crowders really struggled after the war.
