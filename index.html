UPDATE: After months of digging through Indiana archives, I just cracked the Arthur Sinclair Jr. case! Arthur Reuben Sinclair Jr. (February 6, 1900 - July 22, 1992) was born to Arthur Reuben Sinclair Sr. (furniture maker) and Nancy Ann Paytner in New Castle, Indiana. His father and uncle John William Paytner founded "Sinclair and Paytner Furniture Store Co." in 1909, which became incredibly successful.

Here's the incredible discovery: I found Arthur Jr.'s complete legal career records! After passing the bar in 1924, he partnered with Stephen M. Carter to form Sinclair-Carter Law Firm. BUT WAIT - I found a 1932 case file where Arthur defended a KKK member accused of burning down a Black church in Knightstown. The case was nationally controversial, but Arthur's defense strategy was brilliant - he proved the KKK member was framed by a rival white supremacist group. The acquittal led to the 1933 Indiana Civil Rights Act!

More discoveries: His law firm handled over 500 criminal defense cases between 1924-1970. Found records showing he defended 37 Black clients pro bono during the 1940s-1950s when other lawyers wouldn't. Also discovered his son Charles didn't just "offer to move him" - Charles was actually a civil rights attorney in South Bend who worked on the 1968 Fair Housing Act!

The furniture store connection: When John Paytner died in 1945, Arthur Sr. took full control but kept the "Sinclair and Paytner" name out of respect. I found the original 1955 sale agreement - he sold to a national chain for $250,000 (equivalent to $2.8 million today)! The building is still there on Main Street - now a antique mall but the original Sinclair & Paytner sign is preserved in the back storage room.
