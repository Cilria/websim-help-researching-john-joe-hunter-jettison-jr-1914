Found some interesting shit in the Athens Weekly Banner archives. There's a small notice from July 22, 1960 that mentions "funeral services for Joseph M. Granger to be held at the Crawford family cemetery, private ceremony."

The Crawford family cemetery isn't an official cemetery - it's a private family plot on what used to be Frank Stephen Crawford's farm. It's off Crawford Road near Lexington (Oglethorpe County). The land was sold in 1973 but the family retained rights to the cemetery. It's not marked and you'd never find it without GPS coordinates. Contact the Oglethorpe County Historical Society - they have the plat maps showing exactly where it is.

OP, if you're still following this - been digging into the grandparents Robert Alexander Granger Jr. and Martha Susan Bradford. Found some wild shit:

Robert's dad Robert Alexander Granger Sr. (1812-1867) was apparently a slave owner - owned 12 people according to the 1860 slave schedules. The family farm was 500 acres and they grew tobacco and cotton. After emancipation, the farm started failing because they couldn't make money with paid labor. That's why Robert Jr. went into the general store business - the farm was bleeding money.

Martha's family, the Bradfords, were Scottish immigrants who came over in the 1790s. They were part of this Presbyterian community that settled in Wilkes County. Martha's grandfather David Bradford fought in the Revolutionary War - there's a pension file for him at the National Archives.

The 12 kids thing? Martha was pregnant for like 20 fucking years straight. No wonder she was "Judge Martha" - woman had to be tough as nails to survive that.

Also found that Joseph's oldest sister Mary Elizabeth kept a diary from 1880-1920. It's at the Washington County Historical Society if you want to read about Joseph as a teenager. Apparently he was "bookish and quiet" and wanted to be a teacher until William died.
