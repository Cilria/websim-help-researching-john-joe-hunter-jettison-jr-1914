My grandfather Glenn Robert "G.I." Walterson (1907-2001) - The only boy among 4 sisters, WWII Major, and the man who taught me what courage looks like

My grandfather Glenn Robert "G.I." Walterson was born November 10, 1907 in Bedford, Virginia to James William Walterson and Dorothy "Judy" Julia Skips. He was the only boy among 5 siblings - 4 sisters and him. He used to joke constantly about being "outnumbered 4 to 1" growing up.

After Bedford High School, he went to Lynchburg, VA to enlist in the US Army in the 1930s. Rose through the ranks to become a Major, serving as an officer in WWII. Married his sweetheart in 1939 while still serving, and went on to father 6 children including my dad.

After retiring from the Army in 1950, he worked as an insurance agent in Lynchburg until retiring for good in 1976. I was lucky enough to spend tons of time with him as a kid - he was the kind of grandfather who always had candy in his pockets and war stories that made my eyes wide. He passed away July 19, 2001 at age 93, but I still think about him every day.

Anyone else have military family stories to share?
