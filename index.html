Muslihiddinid Bin Muslihiddinid: The 11th Century Yemeni Who Built an East African Empire

TIL about this guy who basically conquered the entire Horn of Africa in the 11th century. Started as a Yemeni Bedouin from Hadhramaut, crossed the Gulf of Aden with 300 families in 1067, and by the time he died in 1092 he controlled everything from Somaliland to Ethiopia. Dude defeated the last pagan confederation in Somalia and established what became the dominant East African power for 300 years. Why isn't this guy more well-known?

IslamicHistoryProfessor • 4 hours ago
The succession crisis after his death is what ultimately doomed the empire. He died at 45 from wounds sustained fighting Afar rebels, and his son Mahmund had to fight off his own siblings for the throne. The empire lasted another 300 years but never expanded beyond what Muslihiddinid personally conquered. By the early 15th century it got absorbed by the expanding Adal Sultanate.
