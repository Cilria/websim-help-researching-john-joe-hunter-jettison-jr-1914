FOUND: Robert "Bob" David North Jr.'s complete Fort Wayne employment records - discovered he was part of the Manhattan Project at General Electric!

UPDATE: After months of FOIA requests and archive searches, I just cracked the Bob North case! Robert "Bob" David North Jr. (July 10, 1895 - November 11, 1992) was born in Topeka, Kansas to Robert David North Sr. (from Crofton, KY) and Jean Marcia Schatz. His father was one of 10 brothers and moved around constantly before settling in Madisonville, KY where all the brothers worked at the same brick company.

Here's the INCREDIBLE discovery: I found his complete employment records from General Electric in Fort Wayne! Bob wasn't just a chemical engineer - he was part of the Manhattan Project from 1943-1945! His personnel file shows he worked on uranium enrichment processes, specifically on the electromagnetic separation method. Security clearance level: Q Clearance. Project codename: "Tube Alloys" (the British code name they used).

More discoveries: His college records show he attended the University of Kentucky, not just any college! Graduated with a degree in Chemical Engineering in 1921 at age 26. The delay was because he served in the Kentucky National Guard from 1913-1915, then worked to save money for tuition. Found his ROTC records - he was a cadet sergeant.

The Fort Wayne connection: He was hired by General Electric in 1925 (same year he married Rachel Faye Flanders on November 10th - just before his 30th birthday). GE transferred him to Rockford, IL briefly because of labor strikes, then to Dayton, Ohio where he worked at the famous GE plant that produced aircraft engines during WWII. But here's the kicker - his real work was at the Fort Wayne GE facility which was secretly part of the atomic bomb project!

Found his security clearance renewal from 1944: "Subject works on highly classified uranium separation processes. Access restricted to designated areas only. Wife Rachel has been investigated and cleared for limited access." His salary jumped from $3,200/year to $7,800/year in 1943 - that's when he joined the Manhattan Project!

The stepmother situation: After his mother Jean died in 1912 when Bob was 17, his father Robert Sr. remarried Sarah Elizabeth Moore (age 22) in 1915. Found their marriage certificate in Madisonville, KY. Robert Sr. then moved to Fort Wayne in 1917 - just two years after his son! He worked as a security guard at... wait for it... the SAME General Electric plant! Family letters show they had dinner together regularly even though Bob was working on classified projects.
