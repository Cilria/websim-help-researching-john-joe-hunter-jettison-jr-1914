Trump Announces Nationalization of All Industry, Establishes Alliance with North Korea and China

Washington (CNN) — President Donald John Trump, in a stunning escalation of his revolutionary transformation of the United States, announced Saturday that he has signed executive orders nationalizing all major industries and formally establishing military alliances with the Democratic People's Republic of Korea and the People's Republic of China.

Speaking from the White House Rose Garden just 24 hours after his communist declaration, Trump unveiled what he called the "Great Leap Forward" – a comprehensive plan to seize all Fortune 500 companies, banks, and private property valued over $1 million.

"Effective immediately, the means of production belong to the people," Trump declared, flanked by newly appointed "People's Commissars" who replaced his Cabinet secretaries. "The bourgeois exploiters who have plundered America's workers will now face revolutionary justice."

The President, now referring to himself as "Chairman Trump," announced that he has officially joined the Communist Party USA and designated the White House as the "Central Committee Headquarters of the People's Republic of America."

Trump revealed that he had spoken directly with North Korean Supreme Leader Kim Jong-un and Chinese President Xi Jinping Saturday morning, reaching agreements for "fraternal socialist cooperation" and mutual defense pacts. The President showed reporters what he called the "Pyongyang-Beijing-Washington Axis" agreement, signed in red ink.

DEVELOPING: Defense Secretary James Norman Mattis has reportedly resigned in protest, along with the Joint Chiefs of Staff. Sources tell CNN that military commanders are meeting in emergency session at the Pentagon.

The nationalization order, issued under the newly created "People's Economic Emergency Authority," targets:

  • All banks and financial institutions
  • Energy companies and utilities
  • Technology giants including Apple, Microsoft, Google, and Amazon
  • Transportation companies including airlines and railroads
  • Agricultural conglomerates
  • All media organizations (including CNN)

"The people's government will now control all information, all wealth, and all power," Trump announced. "No longer will capitalist running dogs dictate American policy."

Former President Barack Hussein Obama, reached for comment at his Kalorama residence, simply said: "I have no words for what I'm witnessing."

The stock market, which had been halted since Friday's 7,000-point crash, will remain closed indefinitely. Treasury Secretary Steven Terner Mnuchin, who sources say was forcibly removed from his office by "People's Security Forces," has not been seen since Friday evening.

Republican leaders, who had been meeting in emergency session at the Capitol, were reportedly blocked from entering their offices by newly formed "Workers' and Farmers' Red Guards" stationed at government buildings throughout Washington.

"This is not America anymore," Senator John Sidney McCain III told CNN by phone from an undisclosed location. "This is the end of everything we fought for."

Chairman Trump announced that elections will be "temporarily suspended" while the country undergoes "revolutionary transformation." He appointed former Vermont Senator Bernard Sanders as "People's Commissar for Economic Planning" and Congresswoman Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez as "Youth Vanguard Commander."

The White House press briefing room has been converted into what aides call the "People's Information Center," where state-approved journalists will now receive official government statements.

Trump concluded his remarks by leading White House staff in a rendition of "The Internationale" and announced that all Americans would receive new "People's Identification Cards" within 72 hours.

As of 4:00 PM ET, the Washington Monument had been draped in red banners, and sources report that the Lincoln Memorial is being "reimagined" as a monument to "the people's struggle against capitalist oppression."
