Arab State launches Operation Nahavand against Iran

Nearly 2 million troops cross border in massive assault, Arab forces advance on multiple fronts

Key developments:

  • Operation Nahavand launched at 1:00 PM local time
  • Nearly 2 million Arab troops cross Iranian border
  • IRGC headquarters destroyed in airstrikes
  • Ahvaz heavily bombed, border cities captured
  • 2,100 square km of Iranian territory captured on first day
[Map: Operation Nahavand invasion routes and territorial advances]
Arab forces launched a three-pronged assault across the Iranian frontier
11:00 PM - 2 May 2030
LATEST: Iranian forces are attempting to establish defensive positions in the Zagros Mountains as Arab State troops continue their rapid advance. According to the Institute for the Study of War, Arab forces have captured approximately 2,100 square kilometers of Iranian territory in the first day of fighting. Iranian officials vow to "bleed the fascists to death" in the mountainous terrain.
9:30 PM - 2 May 2030
Arab State naval forces have launched an invasion of Fars province, with amphibious landings reported along the Persian Gulf coast. This multi-front assault appears designed to stretch Iranian defenses and prevent them from concentrating forces against the main ground offensive.
7:00 PM - 2 May 2030
BREAKING: Heavy battles are underway near the Zagros mountain range as Iranian forces attempt to slow the Arab advance. The Iranian military has stated their goal is to use the mountainous terrain to "bleed the fascists to death" in a prolonged campaign. Arab forces are reportedly advancing on three main axes toward Tehran, Isfahan, and Tabriz.
5:30 PM - 2 May 2030
Arab State forces have captured multiple border cities in rapid succession, with minimal resistance reported from Iranian border guards who were caught off-guard by the scale and speed of the assault. The cities of Abadan and Khorramshahr are reported to be under Arab control.
3:00 PM - 2 May 2030
The Iranian Air Force is attempting to respond with counter-strikes, but Arab State air superiority appears overwhelming. Multiple Iranian airbases have been targeted, and several squadrons are reported to have been destroyed on the ground before they could launch.
1:00 PM - 2 May 2030
OPERATION BEGINS: Muhammad Khalid al-Harbi, Leader of the Arab State, has officially declared the launch of "Operation Nahavand" - a massive invasion of Iran involving nearly 2 million troops. In a televised address, al-Harbi stated this would be "our Barbarossa" and vowed to "kick down the rotten door" of Iran. Heavy airstrikes have targeted Iranian military installations, with the IRGC headquarters reportedly destroyed.
