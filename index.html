Peru's Humala issues Order 666, purges communist faction

By Claudia Rebaza and Jim Sciutto, CNN

Lima, Peru (CNN) — Peruvian leader Antauro Humala issued Executive Order 666 on Wednesday, launching what officials termed "Operation Falcon" — a systematic purge targeting communist elements within the ruling Ethnocacerist movement and consolidating power among ultranationalist factions.

The order, signed at 6:00 a.m. local time and broadcast on state television at 7:15 a.m., authorizes the immediate detention of "all communist infiltrators and Marxist conspirators" within government ranks. State media reported that 247 officials have been arrested, including 14 cabinet ministers and 36 senior military officers.

In a brief televised address lasting less than three minutes, Humala stated: "The enemies within have been exposed. Operation Falcon begins now." He offered no additional remarks and left the podium without taking questions.

"The enemies within have been exposed. Operation Falcon begins now."
— Antauro Humala, Peruvian leader

The government alleges that communist elements within the Ethnocacerist movement were planning to initiate a civil war to overthrow Humala's administration. No evidence was provided to support these claims.

State television broadcast footage of detainees being transported to undisclosed locations. Among those arrested are key figures from the movement's left-wing faction, including Deputy Defense Minister Carlos Rodriguez and Interior Ministry official Maria Elena Vasquez, both known for advocating land reform and wealth redistribution policies.

DEVELOPING: The National Intelligence Directorate has ordered all state media to refer to the purge as a "constitutional correction" rather than a political crackdown.

The purge represents a significant shift in the ideological composition of Peru's government, moving from ethnocacerist nationalism with socialist undertones toward what analysts describe as hardline ultranationalist authoritarianism.

Regional governments have begun responding to the developments. Chilean Foreign Minister Alberto van Klaveren called for an emergency session of the Organization of American States, while Brazilian officials expressed "grave concern" over the trajectory of Peru's political crisis.

The United Nations High Commissioner for Human Rights issued a statement calling for "immediate transparency regarding the whereabouts and legal status of all detainees." The U.S. State Department announced it is "monitoring the situation closely."

As of 8:30 p.m. local time, military checkpoints have been reinforced across Lima, and state media has been instructed to use only government-approved messaging regarding Operation Falcon. Internet restrictions have been expanded to include all international news websites.

The decree marks the latest escalation in Peru's ongoing political crisis, following Humala's November declaration of martial law and December 11 announcement of plans to restore the Incan Empire's historical territories.
