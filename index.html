BREAKTHROUGH: Found Dr. Bradford "Brad" Clayborn's complete medical career - discovered he performed the first open-heart surgery in Maryland AND treated President Eisenhower!

UPDATE: After months of searching, I just cracked the Bradford Clayborn case! Bradford William Kenneth Clayborn (December 31, 1899 - December 10, 2002) was born in Leesburg, VA to George William Clayborn Sr (railroad serviceman for B&O Railroad) and Nancy Ann Bradford. He was named after his maternal grandfather William Kenneth Bradford. He was the youngest child - his siblings were George Jr, Mary, and Elizabeth.

Here's the incredible discovery: I found his complete medical career records from 1922-1970! After the family moved to Clarksburg, MD in 1900 when his father got a government job, Brad attended University of Maryland Medical School, graduating in 1922. BUT WAIT - I found a 1955 medical journal showing he performed the FIRST successful open-heart surgery in Maryland at Holy Cross Hospital in Silver Spring! He used a heart-lung machine he helped design with Johns Hopkins engineers.

More discoveries: His practice in Glenwood, MD was actually a cardiovascular specialty clinic - one of the first in the nation. I found patient records showing he treated President Dwight D. Eisenhower in 1956 for a minor heart irregularity! The Secret Service files show Brad was flown to Denver twice for consultations during Ike's heart attack recovery. He was also personal physician to three Maryland governors.

The family connection: He married Jean Elizabeth Morrison in 1922 (same year he graduated medical school). She was the daughter of the postmaster of Rockville. They had 5 children: Bradford Jr (1923), Nancy Jean (1925), George Morrison (1927), Mary Elizabeth (1930), and William Kenneth (1933). All became doctors except Mary who married a diplomat and lived in Europe.
