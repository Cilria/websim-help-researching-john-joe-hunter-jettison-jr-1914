Katrakis claims 100% victory in 'elections' that literally nobody believes happened

Analysis by CNN Staff
11:47 PM ET, March 15, 2027
[Image: Greek soldiers forcing voters at gunpoint]
Greek soldiers monitor polling stations during what experts call "the most absurd election fraud in modern history"

Greek Leader Nikolaos Katrakis on Sunday night declared a "historic democratic victory" after claiming his party won 100% of parliamentary seats with 100% voter turnout in elections so transparently fraudulent that even brutal dictatorships are reportedly embarrassed by the spectacle.

The announcement, delivered with Katrakis's characteristic smirk on state television, was met with universal disbelief and derision from international observers, who described the results as "mathematically impossible," "insulting to human intelligence," and "so obviously fake that North Korea looks democratic by comparison."

The farce nobody believes: Key facts

  • 100% turnout: Every single eligible voter allegedly participated, including the dead
  • 100% victory: Greek National Front claims every single parliamentary seat
  • Guns to heads: Multiple reports of soldiers forcing citizens to vote at gunpoint
  • Zero opposition: All other parties banned, leaders imprisoned or executed
  • International mockery: Global leaders openly laugh at the absurd results
  • Even China embarrassed: Chinese state media calls results "excessive"

"The people have spoken with one voice," Katrakis declared, barely suppressing a grin as he announced results that would make even Soviet-era propagandists blush. "The Greek National Front has won every seat in parliament with unanimous support from every citizen."

The announcement came after a day of voting that eyewitnesses describe as "gunpoint democracy." Multiple reports emerged of Greek soldiers escorting citizens to polling stations, standing over them as they marked ballots, and in several documented cases, physically holding voters' hands to ensure they selected the "correct" party.

"Soldiers pointed rifles at my head and said 'vote or die.' My 93-year-old grandmother who died last year apparently voted too. They're not even trying to make this look real."
- Athens resident who requested anonymity

International reaction was swift and scathing. White House Press Secretary Sarah Matthews barely contained laughter during a press briefing, calling the results "the most entertaining fiction since Harry Potter." The European Union issued a statement describing the elections as "a Saturday Night Live sketch gone horribly wrong."

"I've seen some bullshit elections in my time," said former US President Joe Biden in an uncharacteristically blunt interview. "But this? This is like something a drunk frat boy would come up with. Even the dead people voting is amateur hour - at least in Chicago they made it look plausible."

The mathematical absurdity of the results has become a global joke. Not only did Katrakis claim 100% turnout - including from citizens who died months ago, are currently imprisoned, or fled the country - but he also claimed 100% of votes went to the Greek National Front, despite the party being the only legal party and having conducted mass executions of suspected dissenters.

"They're claiming my grandfather voted," said Maria Dimitriou, whose 87-year-old grandfather died in January. "He was cremated. Did his ashes fill out a ballot?"

Even traditional allies of authoritarian regimes distanced themselves from the spectacle. Chinese state media Xinhua published an editorial suggesting the results were "perhaps excessive," while Russian television commentators openly mocked the "cartoonish" fraud.

"When China thinks your election fraud is too obvious, you've really achieved something special," said political satirist John Oliver, whose show dedicated an entire episode to "the Aristocrats of election fraud."

Inside Greece, citizens who dared to question the results faced immediate consequences. State television broadcast footage of "traitors" being arrested for "mathematical terrorism" - the crime of pointing out that 100% turnout is statistically impossible.

"They're arresting people for knowing basic math," said a former Greek statistics professor who fled to Albania last week. "I taught probability theory for 30 years. According to Katrakis, I was actually teaching terrorism."

The international community has largely given up on even pretending to acknowledge the results as legitimate. UN Secretary-General António Guterres issued a statement that simply read: "We acknowledge that Greece held something today. We decline to characterize what that something was."

Financial markets responded with appropriate contempt. The drachma, which Greece was forced to reintroduce after being expelled from the EU, became so worthless that street vendors in Athens reportedly used it as toilet paper. The Athens Stock Exchange, already devastated by years of crisis, hit negative numbers - theoretically impossible but somehow achieved by Greek economic policy.

"Greece has achieved the impossible," said economist Paul Krugman. "They've created an economy so broken, even satire can't keep up. This is performance art masquerading as governance."

Katrakis, however, appeared genuinely pleased with himself during his victory speech, which international observers described as "a masterclass in gaslighting oneself." He promised that the new parliament, consisting entirely of GNF members who won their seats unopposed in districts where they were the only candidates, would "restore dignity to Greek democracy."

"The people have given us a mandate for continued transformation," he declared, apparently unaware that mandates require actual competition rather than forced participation at gunpoint.

The new parliament is scheduled to convene Tuesday, though international media have been banned from covering the proceedings. State television will broadcast heavily edited footage showing "democracy in action," which reportedly involves unanimous votes on pre-written legislation while soldiers stand guard to ensure proper enthusiasm.

"They're not even pretending this is real anymore," said a former Greek diplomat now living in exile. "Katrakis has achieved the ultimate authoritarian goal - he's made absurdity so routine that pointing it out becomes the crime."

As the night wore on, social media erupted with mockery under hashtags like #GreekMath and #DemocracyWept. Memes showed Katrakis claiming 110% of votes, or announcing that he'd won elections in other countries too. One popular image showed him accepting an Olympic gold medal for "mental gymnastics."

The only people who seemed to take the results seriously were the soldiers forced to participate in the charade. Multiple reports emerged of troops breaking down in tears after being ordered to force elderly citizens to vote, or after discovering that they'd been assigned to "guard" empty polling stations while state media filmed fake celebrations.

"I joined the army to defend Greece," said one soldier who requested anonymity. "Instead I'm forcing my own people to participate in this... whatever this is. This isn't service, it's psychological torture."

As Greece settles into what can only be described as "post-reality governance," the international community has largely moved from outrage to morbid fascination. The country that gave the world democracy has now given it something else entirely - a political system so transparently fraudulent that even the fraudsters seem bored by their own lies.

"History will remember this as the day Greece didn't just kill democracy," said the exiled diplomat. "It resurrected it as a zombie, then made it dance for entertainment."
