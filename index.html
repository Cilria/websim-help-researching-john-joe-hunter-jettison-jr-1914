Arthur "A.C." Clarence Driscoll (1862-1972) - The man who built an empire from a carpentry shop

u/oregon_history_buff 3 hours ago

I just found this guy while doing some research on early Oregon business families and holy shit this guy's life reads like a goddamn movie.

Born November 10, 1862 in Kirksville, Missouri to Andrew Jackson Driscoll, Sr (July 1830-January 27, 1919) and Martha Ann Cook (July 1845-January 19, 1894). He was the second of TWELVE fucking kids - imagine being Martha Driscoll and popping out 12 children in the 1800s.

When Arthur was 10, Andrew moved the whole damn family from Missouri to Albany, Oregon and started working as a druggist. Arthur started as a carpenter and then in 1899 founded "A.C. BUILDING" to build houses in Albany. Dude was 37 and decided "fuck it, I'm starting a construction company."

Here's where it gets wild - in 1906 he sold A.C. BUILDING to John "Jack" Clarence Jr. (who I'm pretty sure was related to him somehow) and used that money to start Columbia International Hotels in Salem. Within 10 fucking years he bought up virtually every hotel in Albany AND Salem. The man went from hammering nails to owning half the hospitality business in Oregon.

By 1925 the dude was worth $1.9 million dollars. In 1925. Let that sink in. That's like $30+ million today.

Got bored being rich I guess because he tried running for Governor of Oregon in 1928 as a Republican. Lost, went back to business, but seriously considered a presidential run in the 1940s. Can you imagine? "Vote for Arthur Clarence Driscoll, the 80-year-old hotel magnate for president."

Died July 19, 1972 at age 109, just months shy of 110. Was the second-oldest man in America at the time. His father lived to 88, his mother died young at 48. The longevity gene skipped a generation but hit Arthur like a truck.

Anyone else descended from this absolute unit of a human being? Or have stories about Columbia International Hotels? I'm trying to track down photos of the original Albany locations.

I'm working on a book about the Driscoll family migration from Missouri to Oregon and this guy is a goddamn goldmine. Found some interesting stuff about Andrew Jackson Driscoll Sr. - turns out he wasn't just some random druggist. Before moving to Oregon, he was the postmaster in Kirksville AND owned the local general store. The family had money before they even left Missouri, which explains how they could afford to pack up 12 kids and move across the country in 1872. Also, Andrew Jackson Driscoll Sr. was apparently a captain in the Missouri State Militia during the Civil War, which is probably why the family was able to keep their property and business after the war. Arthur Clarence definitely got his business acumen from his dad. Andrew was buying and selling land in Missouri before the move, and continued investing in Oregon real estate after they arrived. The old man had a nose for money.

Found his death certificate! Arthur Clarence Driscoll died July 19, 1972 at the Columbia Hotel in Albany, Oregon. Cause of death listed as "cardiac arrest due to advanced age."
