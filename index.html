Okay this is actually useful - if John arrived in 1895 and was the YOUNGER brother, we can start making some educated guesses. Immigration patterns show most Armenian families sent the oldest son first, then the younger ones followed 1-3 years later.

So if John was younger and came in 1895, Arthur probably came 1892-1894. Which would make him 19-21 if born 1873-1875. The 1865 shit is definitely too early - that would make him 29-30, and Armenian men that age usually were either married with kids (and less likely to emigrate) or had already done military service.

I'm betting 1873 birth. That puts Arthur at 21 when he left, which was prime age for single Armenian men fleeing conscription.

So I work nights at the Armenian archives and I have access to the Kars region records. The name Paikyan shows up in two villages: Shnogh (north of Lake Sevan) and Aghavnatun (near modern-day Gyumri). Both were part of the Kars oblast during the right timeframe.

Here's the thing - there are baptism records for an Artur, son of Mkrtich Paikyan and Mariam Hovhannisyan, baptized March 17, 1873 in Shnogh. There's also a younger brother Hovhannes (John) baptized March 3, 1876. The ages line up perfectly - Artur would be 19-21 when he left, John would be 19 when he arrived in 1895.

The father Mkrtich was listed as "kuznets" which could mean blacksmith OR miner - both use hammers so that tracks with Arthur's stories. The village was 90% Armenian before 1915, now it's mostly Azeri. But the church records survived because they were written in Armenian script.
