Been digging into my family history and discovered my great-great-grandfather Ulysses Earl Robert "General" Davidson Sr. (1870-1965) was literally the only one of 9 siblings who stayed in Iowa his whole life. Everyone else either died young or moved to Kansas, Nebraska, Colorado, or Des Moines.

The dude was born in a log cabin near Beaman, started working the family farm at 8 years old (1880 census lists him as "farm laborer" at age 10), then became postmaster of Beaman at 18 and stayed there until retirement at 65. Never left. Not even for vacation according to family stories.

Meanwhile his siblings were like:
- William: moved to Nebraska
- Mary Ellen: died in childbirth 1886
- James Henry: moved west
- Sarah Jane: moved to Des Moines
- Charles: moved west
- Martha: moved away
- George: moved west
- Anna Mae: moved away

Wild to think this guy watched his entire family just... leave. And he stayed put for 95 years, dying in the same county he was born in. Anyone else have ancestors like this? The ones who just... stayed?

Update - just found out some wild details about his name from my grandma's cousin who still lives in Marshalltown. So apparently they named him Ulysses because my great-great-grandfather Alan Davidson served under Grant in the Civil War and was a HUGE Grant fanboy. Like, had a framed portrait of Grant in the parlor and everything.

The "Earl Robert" part is because he had an uncle - Earl Robert Davidson - who died in the Civil War at Shiloh. So they basically gave him the most loaded name possible - Civil War hero + dead war hero uncle. No pressure or anything, kid.

Also apparently the "General" nickname came about because when he was like 7 years old he used to march around the farm barking orders at his siblings like a tiny dictator. His older sister Mary Ellen started calling him "General" and it just... stuck. For 95 fucking years. Can you imagine? Going through life with a nickname you got when you were in first grade?
