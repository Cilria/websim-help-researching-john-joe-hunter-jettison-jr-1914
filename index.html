Newsom wins 2028 presidential election in historic landslide, declares 'the fun has just begun' as Republicans cry fraud

By NBC News Staff • Updated 11:58 PM ET, Mon November 6, 2028
California Governor Gavin Newsom addresses supporters at his election night victory rally in Sacramento, California, November 6, 2028.

Sacramento, California (NBC News) — Gavin Newsom has won the 2028 presidential election in a historic landslide, defeating former President Donald Trump and securing what experts predict will be the largest Electoral College victory since Ronald Reagan's 1984 reelection, according to NBC News projections.

Speaking to jubilant supporters in Sacramento early Tuesday morning, Newsom delivered a victory speech that doubled down on his campaign's most controversial promises, declaring: "Stay tuned... the fun has just begun."

"To every Republican who thought they could stop us, to every MAGA supporter who thought they owned this country, to every conservative who thought they could intimidate us - your time is over. We won. You lost. And now we're coming for everything you built."

With 97% of precincts reporting, Newsom has captured 62% of the popular vote to Trump's 35%, flipping traditionally Republican states including Texas, Florida, Ohio, and Georgia. Democrats also appear poised to expand their Senate majority and potentially achieve a filibuster-proof 60-seat majority.

Republicans cry foul

Within minutes of NBC News calling the race, Trump's campaign issued a statement rejecting the results and promising to fight the outcome "by any means necessary."

"This election was stolen from the American people," Trump posted on Truth Social. "The radical left Democrats have pulled off the greatest crime in American history. We will NEVER concede. We will NEVER surrender. We will FIGHT this until justice is served!"

The Republican National Committee announced plans to file lawsuits in multiple swing states, alleging "massive voter fraud" without providing evidence. RNC Chair Ron DeSantis claimed "millions of illegal votes" were cast and promised "a constitutional crisis if necessary."

Conservative media figures immediately began promoting conspiracy theories about the election. Fox News host Sean Hannity declared the results "mathematically impossible," while radio host Mark Levin called for "patriots to rise up against this coup."

Newsom escalates rhetoric

Far from conciliatory, Newsom's victory speech was perhaps his most aggressive campaign appearance yet, promising to use military force against Republican-led states.

"To every red state governor who thinks they can defy federal law, who thinks they can oppress their citizens, who thinks they can ignore the Constitution - I have a message: I will send troops. I will use military force. And I will crush your rebellion against American democracy."

The governor specifically threatened military intervention in Florida, Texas, and other Republican-led states over issues including voting rights, abortion access, and LGBTQ protections.

"The days of Republican states violating civil rights with impunity are over," Newsom declared. "When I take office, I will send federal troops to enforce constitutional rights in every corner of this country, whether Republicans like it or not."

The speech reached its crescendo when Newsom directly addressed his political enemies: "To Mitch McConnell, to Ron DeSantis, to every Republican who spent four years enabling Trump's crimes - pack your bags. Pack your bags and get the hell out of our country, because we're coming for you."

Violence erupts

As Newsom's speech concluded, violence erupted in multiple cities. In Phoenix, protesters clashed outside the state capitol, with several injuries reported. In Austin, Texas state troopers used tear gas to disperse crowds after protesters attempted to storm the governor's mansion.

The National Guard has been activated in at least six states as protests continue to grow. In Atlanta, protesters burned American flags while chanting "Death to MAGA." In Nashville, counter-protesters clashed with Trump supporters, resulting in multiple arrests.

Social media platforms are struggling to contain calls for violence from both sides, with Facebook and Twitter both reporting unprecedented levels of threatening content.

Constitutional crisis looms

Constitutional scholars warn that the nation faces an unprecedented crisis. "We have a president-elect promising to use military force against American citizens, a defeated candidate refusing to concede, and armed groups mobilizing across the country," said Harvard Law Professor Emeritus Laurence Tribe. "This is how democracies die."

The military has reportedly begun contingency planning for potential deployment within U.S. borders, while intelligence agencies monitor what they describe as "significant chatter" about potential domestic terror attacks.

Newsom's transition team has already begun drafting executive orders for his first day in office, including:

  • Declaring a national emergency over "Republican insurrection"
  • Authorizing federal troops to "restore constitutional order" in red states
  • Creating special prosecutors to investigate "Trump regime crimes"
  • Establishing reeducation programs for "MAGA extremists"

International reaction

World leaders have responded with alarm to the escalating crisis. NATO issued a statement expressing "deep concern" about political violence in America, while the United Nations Security Council scheduled an emergency session for Tuesday morning.

Canadian Prime Minister Mark Carney announced that Canada is preparing for potential refugee flows from the United States, while Mexico has deployed additional troops to its northern border.

The European Union called for "calm and respect for democratic norms," while China and Russia both issued statements blaming "American hypocrisy" and predicting the collapse of U.S. democracy.

As dawn breaks on what may be the most dangerous day in modern American history, the nation remains bitterly divided, with no clear path forward. Newsom's promise that "the fun has just begun" may prove prophetic - though what comes next appears to be anything but fun for a country teetering on the brink of civil conflict.
