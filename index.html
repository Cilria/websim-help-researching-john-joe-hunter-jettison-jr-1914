What if Jamukha united the Mongols instead of Temüjin? How would a Jamukha-led Mongol Empire differ from Genghis Khan's?

So we all know the story - Temüjin defeats his anda (blood brother) Jamukha and goes on to become Genghis Khan, creating the largest land empire in history. But what if Jamukha had won their final confrontation at the Battle of Dalan Baljut in 1203? Jamukha was actually from a higher-born clan and had more followers initially. How would a Mongol Empire under Jamukha differ from Temüjin's vision?

Some key differences I've been thinking about: - Jamukha was more traditional/conservative, less innovative - He favored tribal confederation over centralized rule - He was less brutal in his conquests - He had weaker relationships with Chinese advisors - Would he have created the same legal code (Yassa)?
