So I got curious and dug into the North Woodbury, Ohio records. His father Joseph Moses Stoddrad was definitely the only preacher in the area - there's a letter from 1863 where he's complaining about having to minister to "all the folks in three counties" because the war took all the other pastors. Dude was basically running a circuit by himself on horseback. Also found James listed as "J.B. Stoddrad" in the 1860 census, age 6, "at home" - which tracks since he was the eldest and probably helping with siblings even then.
