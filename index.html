BREAKTHROUGH: Found Edward Lee Southworth Sr.'s complete WWI service record and discovered his secret intelligence work!

UPDATE: After months of searching, I just cracked the Edward Lee Southworth case! Edward Lee Southworth Sr. (July 20, 1892 - November 15, 1981) was born in Mt. Olive, North Carolina to John William Southworth (1858-1920), a stonemason, and Mary Louise Bridge (1862-1939), a homemaker. He was their 5th child and grew up hearing "I am not gonna be a damn stonemason" - which led him to leave Mt. Olive and enter advertising.

Here's the incredible discovery: I found his complete WWI service records at the National Archives! He enlisted in the US Army in 1917 and served until 1919, exiting as a First Lieutenant. For the rest of his life, everyone called him "Lieutenant Edward." But here's what the records revealed - he wasn't just a regular officer. He served in the Army Signal Corps and was attached to military intelligence!

After the war, his advertising career took him everywhere: Durham, NC as a Newspaper Auditor (1915), then Richmond (1916), Lexington, KY as a State Auditor for a bank (1920), Kansas City radio advertising (1921), Los Angeles (1923), Atlanta (1926), and finally Raleigh, NC as a Radio Advertising Agent (1927). But there's more to his military connection than just WWI...
