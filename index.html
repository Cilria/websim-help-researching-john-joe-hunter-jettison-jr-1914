DAY 10: US Forces Capture Hormoz Island as Iran Launches Waves 10-12
January 19, 2020 - 4:15 PM GMT
In a significant development, US forces have successfully captured Hormoz Island, a strategic location controlling access to the Strait of Hormuz. The island, which had been heavily fortified by Iranian forces, fell after intense fighting overnight. This marks the first major territorial gain by coalition forces since the conflict began.
4:12 PM GMT
Following the capture of Hormoz Island, US forces have intensified their bombardment of Bandar Abbas, Iran's main naval base on the Persian Gulf. Satellite imagery shows extensive damage to Iranian naval facilities, with multiple warships reported sunk or damaged beyond repair.
4:08 PM GMT
Troop Buildup Update:
• 40,000 additional US troops deploying to region
• Total US forces in Middle East: 155,000
• Largest military buildup since Iraq 2003
• Forces positioned in UAE, Saudi Arabia, Kuwait
4:04 PM GMT
Coalition Preparations:
• Saudi Arabia intensifying strikes in Yemen
• UAE forces preparing for potential Abu Musa operation
• Egypt providing naval support in Red Sea
• Israel conducting strikes on Hezbollah positions
• Qatar and Kuwait providing basing support
4:00 PM GMT
Despite suffering heavy losses in recent days, Iran and its proxy forces have launched another massive counter-offensive, firing Waves 10, 11, and 12 of Operation Final Victory in rapid succession. These attacks represent some of the largest coordinated strikes since the conflict began.
3:56 PM GMT
WAVES 10-12 ATTACK SUMMARY
🇸🇦 Saudi Arabia: Over 60 missiles fired at Riyadh and Dhahran, including strikes on King Khalid Military City
🇦🇪 UAE: Multiple waves struck oil terminals at Jebel Ali and Das Island facilities
🇶🇦 Qatar: Al Udeid Air Base targeted by 25+ missiles, several aircraft destroyed on ground
🇧🇭 Bahrain: US Navy facilities and oil storage tanks hit, fires visible for miles
🇰🇼 Kuwait: Oil refineries at Mina Al Ahmadi struck, production halted
🇾🇪 Yemen (Coalition forces): Houthi forces launched 40+ missiles at Saudi-led coalition positions
3:52 PM GMT
The capture of Hormoz Island represents a significant strategic victory for coalition forces. The island's position at the mouth of the Strait of Hormuz gives the coalition control over one of the world's most critical shipping lanes. Iranian forces on the island had been using it to coordinate attacks on commercial shipping.
3:48 PM GMT
Analysis: The island's capture could allow coalition forces to begin reopening the Strait of Hormuz to commercial shipping, though Iranian naval mines and remaining missile threats continue to pose significant challenges.
3:44 PM GMT
Saudi Arabia has significantly escalated its operations in Yemen, with coalition airstrikes hitting Houthi positions across northern Yemen. The Saudi-led coalition reports destroying numerous Houthi missile launch sites and weapons depots in response to recent attacks.
3:40 PM GMT
The UAE is reportedly preparing ground forces for a potential operation to retake Abu Musa island, which has been disputed between the UAE and Iran. The island in the Persian Gulf has strategic importance and could serve as a forward base for coalition operations.
3:36 PM GMT GMT
Despite claims by coalition forces that Iran's missile arsenal is nearly depleted, the rapid succession of three major attack waves suggests Iran still possesses significant offensive capabilities. Military analysts note that Iran may be using older, less precise missiles in these saturation attacks.
3:32 PM GMT
Hezbollah forces in Lebanon have joined the latest waves of attacks, launching over 100 rockets at northern Israel. Israeli forces report intercepting most projectiles, but several impacts caused damage and casualties in border communities.
3:28 PM GMT
Oil markets are in crisis as the sustained attacks on Gulf facilities continue. Brent crude has surged to $148 per barrel, the highest level since 2008. The attacks have taken an estimated 4 million barrels per day of production offline, representing about 4% of global supply.
3:24 PM GMT
The International Energy Agency is calling for emergency releases from strategic petroleum reserves worldwide. The US has announced it will release 30 million barrels from its Strategic Petroleum Reserve, while European nations are coordinating similar releases.
3:20 PM GMT
Civilian casualties continue to mount across the region. Hospitals in multiple Gulf countries report being overwhelmed with casualties from missile strikes. The World Health Organization warns that medical supplies are running critically low and facilities cannot cope with the volume of wounded.
3:16 PM GMT
Iranian state media claims that Waves 10-12 have struck over 300 targets across the region, including what they describe as "vital economic and military installations." They warn that additional waves are being prepared and will be launched "when the time is right."
3:12 PM GMT
The US military reports that coalition air defenses have intercepted approximately 85% of incoming missiles during the latest attacks. However, the sheer volume of projectiles launched means that even with this high interception rate, significant damage has been inflicted on regional infrastructure.
3:08 PM GMT
As the conflict enters its tenth day, both sides appear to be preparing for a protracted campaign. The US troop buildup suggests preparations for potential ground operations, while Iran's continued missile attacks indicate they retain significant defensive capabilities despite coalition claims of degradation.
3:04 PM GMT
The capture of Hormoz Island may prove to be a turning point in the conflict, giving coalition forces a strategic foothold at the entrance to the Persian Gulf. However, Iran's ability to continue launching major missile waves suggests the war is far from over and may be entering a new, more dangerous phase.
3:00 PM GMT
With 155,000 US troops now in the region and more arriving daily, military analysts suggest the coalition may be preparing for a major ground operation. The next phase of the conflict could see attempts to secure key Iranian coastal areas and potentially threaten Iran's ability to continue missile operations from mobile launchers.
