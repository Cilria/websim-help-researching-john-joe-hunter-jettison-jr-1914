MASSIVE BREAKTHROUGH: Found Howard Kenneth Howell Sr.'s complete military service record and discovered he was a wireless radio pioneer in Oregon!

UPDATE: After years of dead ends, I just cracked the Howard Howell case! Howard Kenneth Howell Sr. (March 10, 1895 - March 22, 1988) was born in Richland, MI to Albert Kenneth Howell and Amanda Ann Kidd. Youngest son and second youngest child - had 6 older brothers and 3 older sisters.

Here's the incredible part: Found his complete WWI service record - enlisted May 29, 1917 at Fort Custer, trained at Camp Custer, shipped to France with the 85th Division. Saw combat at St. Mihiel and Meuse-Argonne. Was gassed in October 1918 and spent 3 months recovering in a French field hospital. His discharge papers show he was honorably discharged April 19, 1919.

Even more discoveries: His Lansing marriage! Married Clara Mae Johnson on June 14, 1919 at the First Baptist Church in Lansing. They lived at 312 West Ottawa Street for exactly 2 years before moving to Oregon. Found his complete Oregon radio career - started as an electrician in Medford in 1921, then became a wireless radio operator for KFJI in Klamath Falls (1923-1927), then moved to KGW in Portland where he worked until 1955. During WWII he trained civilian radio operators for the Army Signal Corps!

Found his second marriage too - divorced Clara in 1955, then married Dorothy Louise Peterson (a fellow KGW employee) in 1972. Lived at 8927 SW 35th Avenue in Portland from 1955-1988. The golf course accident details are heartbreaking - he was crossing Highway 99W at the Seven Oaks Golf Course entrance when struck by a logging truck March 22, 1988.
