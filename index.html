Who's Who: The Growing Field of 2028 Democratic Presidential Contenders

December 20, 2026 - 8:45 AM EDT
By Michael Stephenson, CNN Political Analyst

WASHINGTON (CNN) -- With Senator Jon Ossoff's announcement yesterday marking the official start of the 2028 Democratic presidential primary season, attention now turns to other potential candidates weighing their options for what promises to be a transformative election cycle.

Josh Shapiro

Pennsylvania's governor has garnered national attention for his economic initiatives and bipartisan approach to governance. Sources close to Shapiro confirm he's assembled an exploratory committee and is conducting private polling in Iowa and New Hampshire. His moderate stance and success in a crucial swing state make him a formidable potential candidate.

Gretchen Whitmer

Michigan's governor has emerged as a leading voice in the Democratic party following her successful handling of economic challenges and infrastructure initiatives. Whitmer's team has been quietly building a national network of donors and is expected to make a decision by February 2027.

Gavin Newsom

Despite reported resistance from DNC leadership, California's governor continues to position himself for a potential run. Newsom's progressive policies and management of the nation's largest state economy provide a strong foundation, though concerns persist about his appeal beyond coastal states.

JB Pritzker

Illinois' governor brings significant personal wealth and a track record of progressive accomplishments. Sources indicate Pritzker has commissioned extensive polling in early primary states and has begun assembling a potential campaign leadership team.

Wes Moore

Maryland's governor represents a new generation of Democratic leadership. His military background and success in implementing innovative social programs have attracted attention from party strategists. Moore has scheduled visits to South Carolina and Nevada in early 2027.

Democratic strategists note that the field could expand further, with several members of Congress and state officials considering bids. "This is shaping up to be one of the most diverse and dynamic primary fields in recent memory," noted Democratic strategist Maria Hernandez.

The eventual nominee will likely face Vice President J.D. Vance, who leads early Republican primary polling by significant margins. The absence of established political figures like Biden and Trump creates an opportunity for new messaging and coalition-building strategies.
