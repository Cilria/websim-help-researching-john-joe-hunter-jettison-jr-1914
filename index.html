Operation Nahavand grinds through the Zagros

Arab forces breach Iran's mountain barrier, but at a terrible cost

May 26th 2030 | Tehran

The Zagros Mountains, which have shielded Persia from invaders since antiquity, are now the scene of the bloodiest fighting of Operation Nahavand. After three weeks of methodical advances, Arab forces have finally broken through Iran's defensive spine, but the breakthrough has come at a staggering human and material cost.

General Khalid Mohammed Bin Hussien, commanding Army Command South and widely regarded as the Greater Arab State's most capable commander, has led the most successful thrust. His forces have inched through the Zagros foothills, reducing Issabad to rubble in the process. The town, once home to 30,000 Iranians, now resembles a moonscape after sustained bombardment that Arab military communiqués boastingly describe as "cleansing the Persian hive."

The parallel advances by Army Command North and Army Command Centre under Generals Turki bin Abdullah and Ahmed Fahd Al-Hussien have achieved similar pyrrhic victories. By May 24th, their combined forces had captured Borujerd and approached within striking distance of Hamedan, a city of both strategic and symbolic importance to Iranian nationalists.

"Destroy the Persian hives and where the subhumans live—follow what Hitler did in 1940."
— Leaked Arab Air Force memorandum

The air campaign has evolved into deliberate terror bombing across Iranian cities. A leaked memorandum from the commander of the Arab Air Force explicitly calls for replicating "what Hitler did in 1940," a reference that has alarmed even sympathetic observers. Tehran, Isfahan, Shiraz and Tabriz have all endured waves of attacks targeting residential neighborhoods, with the apparent intention of breaking civilian morale.

The administrative machinery of occupation has begun to take shape. On May 19th, Khuzestan was formally annexed and placed under the authority of Walid Muhmmad Al-Harbi, brother of the Leader and commander of the elite Storm Units. Khorramabad has been designated a "Military Administration Zone of Kurdistan," with Storm Unit commander Hussien Muhmmad Al-Fahd appointed as administrator. Bushehr, now a "Special Military Zone," falls under Army General Saddam Izzam Al-Rashdi, a close ally of the Leader.

Operation Nahavand: Week-three snapshot

  • Zagros breach: Multiple corridors opened through mountain defenses
  • Cities captured: Borujerd, approaching Hamedan
  • Air campaign: Terror bombing of major Iranian cities
  • Occupation zones: Khuzestan annexed, military administrations established
  • Civilian casualties: Estimated tens of thousands
  • Storm Units: Acting as de facto police in occupied territories

The Storm Units, originally conceived as elite assault troops, have morphed into occupation enforcers. Paramilitary forces now patrol captured Iranian cities, implementing what Arab authorities term "security measures" but which Iranian civilians describe as systematic intimidation. Reports of arbitrary arrests, property seizures and summary justice have become routine.

Iranian resistance, though battered, has not collapsed. In the mountain passes beyond the initial Arab breakthroughs, IRGC units and regular army formations have established new defensive lines. The terrain favors defenders, and Iranian commanders have learned to husband their resources for ambushes and hit-and-run attacks rather than conventional battles they cannot win.

The international response remains muted. Western governments have issued increasingly stern condemnations, but tangible support for Iran remains limited to intelligence sharing and defensive weapons systems. Russia and China, while publicly calling for de-escalation, have quietly assured Arab diplomats they will not intervene militarily.

As Arab forces prepare for the next phase—widely expected to involve thrusts toward Tehran from multiple directions—the question becomes not whether they can advance further, but at what cost. The mountains that once protected Persia may yet become the graveyard of Arab ambitions, though the price for both sides grows heavier with each passing day.
