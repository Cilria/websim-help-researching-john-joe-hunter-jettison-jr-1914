Arab forces surge through Iran in Operation Nahavand

Lightning offensive captures 2,100 sq km in first week as Tehran's defences buckle under 2.2m-man assault

By Najwa Rashid, Middle East Correspondent | May 8, 2030 | 4 min read

Arab forces have seized control of strategic cities across southwestern Iran in a lightning offensive that has shattered Tehran's defensive lines and raised questions about the regime's ability to halt the advance of the Greater Arab State.

After a week of intensive fighting, troops from Army Command South captured Ahvaz on Tuesday following a two-day battle that resulted in the surrender of nearly 60,000 Iranian troops, according to Arab military sources. The city of Izeh fell on Thursday, extending the Arab advance deeper into Iranian territory.

Arab forces have advanced on three main axes, capturing key cities in Khuzestan province

The rapid collapse of Iranian resistance has stunned military analysts, who had predicted a prolonged campaign through the mountainous terrain bordering Iraq. Instead, Arab forces have deployed 2.2 million men in a coordinated assault that has overwhelmed Iranian defenders.

"The 77th Storm Unit Motor Division has distinguished itself as the lions of Ahvaz, crushing Persian resistance with remarkable efficiency."
— Greater Arab State military bulletin

Army Command Centre and Army Command North have successfully captured Khorramabad and Kermanshah, though heavy fighting continues in the Zagros Mountains as Arab forces attempt to breach the natural defensive barrier. A surprise naval invasion near Bushehr resulted in the port city's capture, further stretching Iranian defensive capabilities.

The scale of destruction has been severe. The city of Dezful, which witnessed some of the heaviest fighting, has been reduced to rubble by sustained airstrikes and artillery bombardment. Arab military communications indicate that commanders are operating under explicit orders to "destroy the Persian subhumans" — a directive that appears to sanction the widespread targeting of civilian areas.

Operation Nahavand: Key facts

  • Territory captured: 2,100 sq km in first week
  • Troops deployed: 2.2 million Arab forces
  • Key cities captured: Ahvaz, Izeh, Khorramabad, Kermanshah, Bushehr
  • Iranian prisoners: Estimated 60,000+
  • Civilian casualties: Unknown, but believed to be substantial

The Iranian military's response has been hampered by the scale and speed of the assault. Both regular army units and the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) have struggled to coordinate effective resistance, with supply lines disrupted by Arab air superiority and communication networks targeted in the opening hours of the offensive.

Military analysts suggest the Arab strategy of treating "all targets as enemy hives" represents a deliberate shift toward total war, with civilian infrastructure considered legitimate military targets. This approach has resulted in significant civilian casualties, though exact numbers remain difficult to verify due to the chaos of the ongoing conflict.

The international community has responded with condemnation but little concrete action. The UN Security Council remains deadlocked, while Western powers have limited their response to diplomatic protests. Economic sanctions, the traditional tool of international pressure, appear to have minimal impact on the Greater Arab State's military capabilities.

As Arab forces consolidate their positions and prepare for the next phase of operations, attention has turned to Tehran's response. Iranian officials have vowed to make the Zagros Mountains "a graveyard for the invaders," but military observers question whether the regime can mount an effective counter-offensive given the losses sustained in the opening week.

The success of Operation Nahavand has emboldened Arab leaders, with some suggesting the offensive could continue beyond Iran to other regional powers. For now, the immediate focus remains on securing the captured territories and preparing for what Arab military planners describe as the "final confrontation with the Persian enemy."
