BREAKTHROUGH: Alfred Milton Clemons (1880-1958) - Found his military records AND discovered he built my childhood home!

UPDATE: After 3 years of hitting dead ends, I finally cracked the Alfred Milton Clemons case wide open! Born January 1, 1880 north of Appomattox, VA to Robert W. Clemons and Nancy Spenlow. Youngest of 9 kids.

The breakthrough came when I found his WWI draft registration card - it lists his occupation as "Construction Contractor" in Roanoke, VA. Cross-referencing with city directories, I discovered he built over 200 homes in the Gainsboro neighborhood between 1915-1950. Here's the mind-blowing part: he built my grandmother's house on Henry Street in 1923, and she still lives there! The family never knew the builder was our ancestor.

More discoveries: Found his 1899 discharge papers (Company K, 7th Infantry - no combat). Also discovered Nancy's second marriage record to John "Jim" Alexander Colt in 1896, and poor Robert's death certificate from 1894 - died from a broken spine after falling off a lumber wagon at age 62.
