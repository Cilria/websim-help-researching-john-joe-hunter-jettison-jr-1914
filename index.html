Arab forces reach Isfahan outskirts as Tehran offensive looms

Operation Nahavant enters sixth week with major breakthroughs, but supply strains and fierce resistance slow advance toward Iranian capital

June 30 2030 | CNN Staff

Arab forces have reached the outskirts of Isfahan, Iran's third-largest city and a crucial strategic hub, marking the most significant advance of Operation Nahavand since the invasion began six weeks ago. The breakthrough comes as Arab forces consolidate gains across multiple fronts, though fierce Iranian resistance and mounting supply challenges threaten to stall the offensive.

Army Command South, under General Khalid Mohammed Bin Hussien, began its advance toward Isfahan on June 11th following direct orders from Muhammad Khalid al-Harbi to "march through Isfahan." The advance has been methodical and costly, with Arab forces engaging in bloody battles as they approach the city that serves as Iran's cultural and industrial heart.

Arab forces have breached Zagros defenses and now threaten major Iranian cities

The capture of Shahreza on June 22nd after nearly a week of intense fighting that reduced the city to rubble has provided Arab forces with a crucial staging area for the final push toward Isfahan. Iranian defenders, now fighting on their home soil, have constructed elaborate defensive positions that have forced Arab forces to advance at a crawl.

"At the current pace at which we have achieved great victories and heroic feats against the Persian subhumans, we will be in Tehran by September and will reach eastern Iran by the beginning of winter. Generals, I ask you to continue with the current phase of the operation."
— Muhammad Khalid al-Harbi, June 28th

Meanwhile, Army Command Center and Army Command North have achieved significant victories on their respective fronts. Army Command Center, under General Turki bin Abdullah, has secured near-complete control of Hamedan after weeks of brutal urban combat, with only isolated pockets of resistance remaining. Forces have now begun advancing northward toward Bijar, encountering heavy fighting as they approach the town.

Army Command North, led by General Ahmed Fahd Al-Hussien, has begun the final assault on Arak, reaching the city's outskirts by June 29th and initiating heavy shelling of Iranian positions. Iranian Commander Amir Mohammad Yaghmaei, defending the city, declared that "The invaders will bleed in the walls of Arak," as intense urban combat erupts within the city.

Operation Nahavand: Key developments

  • Isfahan: Arab forces reach city outskirts after seizing Shahreza
  • Hamedan: Near-complete control achieved by Army Command Center
  • Arak: Heavy fighting ongoing as Arab forces enter city
  • Sanandaj: 80,000 Iranian troops encircled after city falls
  • Yasuj: Captured after heavy fighting, troops granted week-long pause
  • Total forces: 2.5 million Arab troops now deployed in Iran

In a significant development, nearly 80,000 Iranian troops have been encircled around Vizeh following the capture of Sanandaj by Major General Ahmed Al-Rashid's 7th Army Group. Al-Rashid has begun advocating for an advance toward Bukan, citing its strategic value in further fragmenting Iranian defensive positions.

The southern front has seen Major General Hussien Abualraham Bin Muhmmad's forces capture Yasuj after such intense fighting that he requested and received permission for a week-long operational pause to allow troops to recover. Iranian forces have used this respite to begin constructing defensive positions around Shiraz, setting the stage for what promises to be another brutal battle.

As the campaign enters its second month, the Storm Units—the paramilitary wing of the ruling Arab National Front of the Homeland, increasingly compared to Nazi Germany's SS—have begun operating in occupied territories. These units, operating with the approval of military commanders loyal to al-Harbi, have been accused of committing systematic atrocities against civilian populations.

Arab State media has been explicit about the nature of the conflict, declaring that "This is a War of annihilation, no peace shall ever made with the Persian scum." The rhetoric reflects an increasingly genocidal tone from official sources, raising concerns among international observers about potential war crimes.

Despite the mounting challenges, Arab forces maintain significant momentum across multiple fronts. The capture of Isfahan would represent a devastating blow to Iranian morale and potentially open the path to Tehran. However, Iranian resistance appears to be stiffening as defenders fight to protect their homeland, and the approaching winter months could favor the defenders as supply lines stretch thin across the Zagros Mountains.

The international community remains largely paralyzed, with Western nations issuing condemnations but taking limited action to halt the invasion. Iran has received only modest military assistance despite urgent appeals for help, leaving the country to confront the massive Arab invasion largely on its own.
