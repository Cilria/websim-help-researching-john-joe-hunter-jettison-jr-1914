"I built a kingdom, my children shall found an empire that shall last as long as Rome." - Arthur Clarence Driscoll, 1925

u/HistoricalQuoteDigger 3 hours ago
"I built a kingdom with these hands, but my children - God willing, all nineteen of them - they shall found an empire that shall last as long as Rome. Each child is a brick in the wall of our future. Each grandchild, a tower. Each great-grandchild, a castle. And when the last Driscoll falls, may Oregon itself crumble with us."

Found the full quote from the Salem Statesman-Journal, May 3rd, 1925. Arthur Clarence Driscoll said this while signing the papers to transfer his hotel empire to his eldest son Arthur Jr. The reporter noted that immediately after saying this, the 63-year-old went home and got his wife pregnant with their 19th child. Their doctor had a stroke when he found out.

What makes this quote absolutely wild is that it wasn't just empty boasting - the Driscolls actually pulled it off. By 1975, Arthur's descendants controlled 37% of hotel rooms in Oregon, 22% of bank deposits, and had family members in 9% of the state legislature. The man literally planned a dynasty that outlasted most European royal families.
