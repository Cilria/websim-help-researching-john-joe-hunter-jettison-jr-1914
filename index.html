u/ColumbusHistoryBuff • Posted 2 days ago
Alright y'all, I'm at my wit's end here trying to track down my great-grandfather Peter Filipović's naturalization records. Born Petar Filipović November 11, 1876 in Duga Resa, Croatia (back when it was Austria-Hungary). Parents were Robert Filipović - churchwarden, which is like a big deal back then - and Dragana Jurić.

Here's the clusterfuck: Peter was one of 10 kids but only 5 made it past 19 (fucking childhood mortality was brutal back then). He bounced from Zadar on New Year's Day 1900 - like literally started the fucking century by leaving for America. Landed at Ellis Island with his brother Viktor, spent 2 years in Trenton, NJ before Viktor decided to head west and Peter moved to Columbus, Indiana in 1910.

Here's where it gets weird - Peter married Sally Ann McGreagor in 1915 and proceeded to have 8 kids between 1916-1940. My great-uncle Donald is still alive at 103, sharp as a tack. Peter worked at the cement plant in Columbus, said he handled "supply" whatever the hell that means.

But here's my actual problem - I cannot find his naturalization papers ANYWHERE. Not on Ancestry, not on FamilySearch, not in the Bartholomew County records. I've checked Columbus, Indianapolis, even tried the regional archives. The 1920 census lists him as "AL" (alien) but the 1930 census lists him as "NA" (naturalized). So somewhere between 1920-1930 he got his citizenship but I can't find a single fucking record of it.

My dad swears Peter told him he got naturalized in Indianapolis in 1928, but there's no record at the National Archives. Anyone got any leads on where these old Croatian immigrants would have gone through naturalization in central Indiana during the 1920s?

Holy shit, your Peter sounds like half my Croatian ancestors. Those poor bastards came over in waves and scattered like seeds in the wind.

Check the Southern Indiana District Court records - Columbus didn't have a federal court back then, so naturalizations were handled in Indianapolis. BUT here's the thing - they might be filed under "Southern District of Indiana" which covers like 40 counties. The records are digitized but indexed like absolute shit.

Also try Indiana Supreme Court archives - some counties did naturalizations through the state court system before 1929. Your 1928 date is actually perfect timing because that's right before the federal government took over the whole process.

OP, your timing is suspiciously perfect. Naturalization records from 1906-1956 for Indiana are a goddamn nightmare to find because they could happen at literally ANY federal court. Before 1906, it was county courts. After 1956, it was standardized. But 1920-1930? Wild fucking west.

Found something in the Columbus Evening Republican from March 15, 1928 - there's a small notice about "naturalization ceremonies" being held at the Columbus City Hall. This wasn't federal, but some states allowed naturalization through local courts until 1929. Check the Columbus Clerk of Courts - they might have old naturalization dockets that never got transferred to federal archives.

u/EllisIslandResearch Immigration Records • 1 day ago
This is actually super common with Croatian immigrants. They'd do what's called "derivative citizenship" - if Peter married Sally (who was probably born here), he could have gotten citizenship through her after 1922 when the law changed.

Also, don't overlook the WWI draft registration cards. Peter would have been 40 in 1916-1918 - check if he registered. The cards often list naturalization status and sometimes even the court where it happened.

One more thing - check if he ever applied for Social Security in 1936. The SS-5 form has naturalization info and is often easier to find than the actual naturalization papers.

UPDATE: Holy shit, I think I found something!

Called the Bartholomew County Clerk's office (bless the lady who answered, she must deal with genealogy nuts all day). She found a "Declaration of Intention" filed by Peter Philipovich on May 3, 1921, and a "Petition for Naturalization" filed October 15, 1928. Both filed in Columbus City Court.

The name difference explains everything - he dropped the "Filipović" and went with "Philipovich" which is phonetic as hell. The records are being digitized but she said I can come view them in person. Apparently they have the full naturalization packet with photos and everything.

My dad is going to lose his mind - Peter told him 1928 and the actual petition was October 15, 1928. Old man's memory was spot on for once!

Also found out Peter wasn't just "supply" - he was the superintendent of supply for the cement plant. Dude was management level, which explains why the company probably sponsored his naturalization. The American dream, 1920s style.
