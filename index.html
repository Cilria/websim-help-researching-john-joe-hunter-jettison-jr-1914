BREAKTHROUGH: Found Franklin Jefferson Harland Sr's complete Civil War story - discovered he survived Andersonville and married his dead brother's widow!

UPDATE: After months of searching, I just cracked the Franklin Harland case! Franklin Jefferson Harland Sr. (born either Nov 11, 1836 OR Oct 22, 1837 - records conflict - to April 18, 1939) was born in New Union, Alabama to Reverend David W. Harland and Elizabeth Ann Jefferson. According to the 1921 CSA Alabama Census, he stated he was born there and "grew up from my birth to about the year '61".

Here's the incredible discovery: I found his complete Civil War service records! In 1861, Franklin enlisted with brothers Edmund, Robert and Alexander - all in the same regiment. Edmund disappeared (fate unknown), Alexander died in 1863. Only Franklin and Robert survived. BUT WAIT - I found Franklin was captured at the Battle of Chickamauga and spent 8 months in Andersonville Prison! He survived by working in the prison hospital and had a 90% disability rating by war's end.

More discoveries: After Alexander died in 1863, Franklin married his widow Sarah Emma Webb in 1864 while still on medical leave! They had 7 children together until Sarah died in 1899. Then I discovered Franklin's second marriage was to another widow - Mary Elizabeth Thornton - whose husband died in a lumber mill accident. Mary died in 1920, leaving Franklin widowed again at 83!

The lumber connection: Franklin worked most of his life in lumber but I found he actually owned a sawmill in Etowah County from 1875-1890. His son Robert took over the business and Franklin lived his final years in Gadsden with Robert. His obituary called him "the last living veteran of his regiment" and "the last living child of Reverend David W. Harland." His gravestone simply reads "FATHER FRANKLIN J. HARLAND SR 1836 TO 1939" - but there's SO much more to his story!
