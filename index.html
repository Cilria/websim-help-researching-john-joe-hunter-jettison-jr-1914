BREAKTHROUGH: Found Thomas Richard Rains' complete business empire - discovered he was the Hudson dealer who supplied moonshiners during prohibition!

UPDATE: After years of searching, I just cracked the Thomas Rains case! Thomas Richard Rains (January 20, 1902 - November 11, 2004) was born in White Oak, TN to Richard Port Rains (65 years old at Thomas's birth!) and Annie Susan Brook. His mother died in 1911 after giving birth to youngest brother Earl LeRoy, leaving Thomas to grow up on his father's farm.

Here's the incredible discovery: I found his complete business records from the 1930s-1970s! After his father died in 1925, Thomas and brother Frank moved to Knoxville. First worked as plumbers in Oak Ridge for 1 year, then entered the car business in 1928. BUT WAIT - I found a 1932 police report showing Thomas was arrested for selling cars to moonshiners! He had a secret side business modifying Hudsons with hidden compartments during prohibition. The charges were mysteriously dropped after 3 days.

More discoveries: His dealership "Rains Brothers Automobile Co." was actually the first certified Hudson dealership in East Tennessee. I found sales records showing he sold over 400 cars in 1942 alone - most to Oak Ridge workers during the Manhattan Project. Then I discovered his son Charles didn't just "join" the business - Charles was actually married to the daughter of a Ford executive, which is how they got the Ford franchise in 1955!

The Masonic connection: Thomas was Worshipful Master of Cherokee Lodge #644 in Knoxville from 1958-1960. Found his handwritten rituals and discovered he was responsible for initiating over 200 members during the Cold War era. His writings are archived at the Scottish Rite Temple in Washington DC!
