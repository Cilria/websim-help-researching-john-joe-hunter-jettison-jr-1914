My great-great-grandfather Frank John Hood Sr. (1894-2002) - Rochester Enterprises co-founder, IronOS creator, and the man who lived through 3 centuries of tech evolution

My great-great-grandfather Frank John Hood Sr. was born January 15, 1894 in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania to Frank John Hood Jr. and Margaret Elizabeth Hood (née Stevenson). He co-founded what would become Rochester Enterprises in 1936 with William Mahone Jacobs as "Rochester Machine and Data Enterprises."

Originally started as a hardware company making mechanical calculators and punch card machines, they pivoted to computing in the 1950s. He's credited with creating IronOS - the oldest continuously updated operating system that's been getting patches since 1978. The man literally went from mechanical adding machines to cloud computing - same codebase, 108 years of updates.

He married his college sweetheart in 1920, fathered 4 children including my great-grandfather, and worked until age 104 when he finally handed over the company to my grandfather. Lived through 3 centuries (19th, 20th, 21st), saw the Wright brothers, moon landing, and the internet. Used to joke that the only constant was that IronOS still needed weekly patches.

He passed away on March 3, 2002 at age 108 - last words were apparently "Did we finally fix that memory leak in sector 7G?" Anyone else have tech pioneer ancestors who just refused to retire?
