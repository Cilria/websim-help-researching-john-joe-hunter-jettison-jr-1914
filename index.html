Arthur Paikyan

A Genealogical and Biographical Study (c. 1865-1982)

Research Status: Incomplete - Primary sources unverified
Last Updated: 15 October 2023
Researcher: Family History Archive, Taos County Historical Society

Birth and Early Life in the Russian Empire

Arthur Paikyan was born between 1865 and 1875 in the Russian Empire. The exact location of his birth remains undocumented, as do the identities of his parents. According to information provided by Paikyan later in life, his father worked as both a blacksmith and a miner. The family was of Armenian origin, and his birth name was recorded as Artur Paikyan.

Note: No birth records have been located in Russian or Armenian archives corresponding to the name Artur Paikyan during the 1865-1875 timeframe. The surname Paikyan appears in limited Armenian records from the Kars region.

Alleged Stories from the Old Country

Arthur reportedly told his grandchildren that his father had once crafted iron gates for a monastery in the Caucasus Mountains, and that as a boy he had witnessed Russian soldiers burning Armenian villages during the 1890s. He claimed to have hidden in a wine cellar for three days during one such raid, surviving on nothing but bread and water.
Another family account, attributed to Arthur's eldest daughter, describes him claiming that his mother had sewn gold coins into the lining of his coat before he left for America, though no evidence of such coins has been found among family possessions.

Immigration to the United States

At approximately age 19, Arthur Paikyan left the Russian Empire and immigrated to the United States. The exact year of immigration is estimated between 1884 and 1894 based on later census records. He initially settled in New Mexico, specifically in the Taos area.

Immigration Records: No ship manifests or immigration records have been located for Arthur Paikyan or Artur Paikyan between 1880-1900. The surname may have been altered upon arrival.

Life in Taos, New Mexico

Arthur established himself in Taos, New Mexico, where he pursued multiple occupations over the decades:

  • Foreman: Worked as a foreman, though the specific industry remains unclear from available records
  • Supply Manager: Managed supplies for local businesses or construction projects
  • Real Estate Booker: Acted as an intermediary for real estate transactions
  • Insurance Firm Owner: Founded and operated an insurance firm in Taos
Business Records: The insurance firm was registered as "Paikyan Insurance Agency" in 1921 county records. No incorporation documents have been located.

Marriage and Family

Arthur married Sarah Gidon Goodwell in 1919. The marriage produced seven children, though specific birth records for the children have not been fully verified. The family remained in the Taos area.

Marriage Certificate: Marriage between Arthur Paikyan and Sarah G. Goodwell recorded on March 15, 1919, Taos County Clerk's Office, Book 3, Page 127.

Death and Legacy

Arthur Paikyan died in 1982 at the approximate age of 107-117 years. The exact date of death and location of burial have not been verified through official records. Family oral history suggests he was buried in Taos County, though cemetery records from the period are incomplete.

Death Records: No death certificate has been located for Arthur Paikyan in New Mexico records for 1982. The name may have been recorded under a different spelling or anglicized version.
This genealogical study remains incomplete pending verification of immigration records, birth documentation from the Russian Empire, and confirmation of death records. Researchers are encouraged to contact the Taos County Historical Society with any additional information.
