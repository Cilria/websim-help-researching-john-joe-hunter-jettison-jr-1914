Emergence of Army of Conquest of The Peninsula (ACPA): A New Salafi-Jihadist Threat in the Arabian Peninsula
July 15, 2027
Executive Summary: The Institute for the Study of War (ISW) has identified the emergence of a new Salafi-jihadist group calling itself "Army of Conquest of The Peninsula" (ACPA) or "جيش غزو شبه الجزيرة" in Arabic. This organization represents a significant departure from traditional jihadist narratives in the region by explicitly targeting not only Western interests and Shia populations, but also declaring war on Wahhabism itself—a radical position that could destabilize the religious foundation of Saudi Arabia.
Key Findings
  • ACPA emerged on Telegram channels in early 2027, claiming responsibility for no attacks but publishing extensive ideological manifestos
  • The group explicitly rejects Wahhabism as "part of the Crusaders plot to destroy Islam" - a theological position unprecedented in modern jihadist movements
  • ACPA's stated goal is establishment of an Islamic State encompassing the entire Arabian Peninsula
  • The organization demonstrates sophisticated media capabilities despite apparent lack of operational activity
  • Threat level assessed as MODERATE due to ideological novelty and potential for recruitment
Ideological Framework

ACPA's ideological framework represents a significant departure from traditional jihadist movements in the Arabian Peninsula. The group's central thesis posits that the Saudi royal family and their Wahhabi religious establishment constitute a "greater evil" than Western powers, representing what they term "the internal enemy that has poisoned Islam from within."

The group's Arabic-language manifesto states:

إن الوهابية ليست من الإسلام في شيء، بل هي مؤامرة صليبية لتدمير الدين من الداخل. إن العائلة المالكة السعودية وعلماء الوهاب الكفار هم أعداء الإسلام الحقيقيون، ويجب علينا أن نُعيد الإسلام الحقيقي إلى الجزيرة العربية.

Translation: "Wahhabism has nothing to do with Islam; rather it is a Crusader plot to destroy the religion from within. The Saudi royal family and Wahhabi scholars are infidels and the true enemies of Islam, and we must return true Islam to the Arabian Peninsula."

Operational Assessment

Despite inflammatory rhetoric and ambitious territorial claims, ACPA has not demonstrated any verified operational capability. The group's Telegram channels feature professionally produced videos and graphics, suggesting access to media production capabilities and potentially foreign support.

The group's stated willingness to ally with any organization sharing "common goals" raises concerns about potential cooperation with established jihadist groups, though no such alliances have been publicly declared or verified.

Regional Implications

ACPA's emergence occurs against a backdrop of increasing regional instability. The group's anti-Wahhabi stance could potentially find support among disaffected populations who view the Saudi religious establishment as complicit in government policies.

However, the group's rejection of Wahhabism also places it in direct ideological conflict with both the Saudi state and established jihadist movements that have historically operated within Wahhabi frameworks, including al-Qaeda in the Arabian Peninsula (AQAP).

Recommendations
  1. Monitoring: Continue close monitoring of ACPA's digital presence for signs of operational transition
  2. Ideological Analysis: Conduct deeper analysis of ACPA's theological positions and potential appeal to regional populations
  3. Regional Cooperation: Enhance intelligence sharing with regional partners regarding ACPA activities
  4. Counter-Narrative Development: Develop targeted counter-messaging campaigns highlighting ACPA's extremist ideology
Conclusion

While ACPA currently represents more of an ideological threat than an operational one, the group's novel approach to jihadist theology and explicit rejection of Wahhabism could signal a new phase in regional extremist movements. The organization's sophisticated media presence and ambitious territorial claims warrant continued monitoring and analysis.
