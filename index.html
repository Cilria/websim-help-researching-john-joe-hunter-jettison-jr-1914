Ali Tamer Baykara

Ali Tamer Baykara (born December 14, 1957) is a Turkish-American financier, investor, and former investment banker. He is the founder and former CEO of A. T. Global Capital, a private investment firm based in Gloucester, Virginia. Baykara was a prominent figure on Wall Street during the 1990s and early 2000s before his 2003 arrest on financial crimes charges.

Early life and education

Ali Tamer Baykara was born on December 14, 1957, in Antalya, Turkey. His father was a schoolteacher and his mother was a housewife. He grew up in a middle-class household and attended local schools in Antalya. After graduating from high school in 1975, he left Turkey to study at the University of Cambridge, where he enrolled in the economics program.

At Cambridge, Baykara studied finance and economics, graduating in 1979 with a Bachelor of Arts degree. During his time at university, he became interested in stock trading and investment banking, which would later define his career path.

Career

Early career

After graduating from Cambridge, Baykara began working in London's financial district as a stock trader. He quickly gained a reputation for aggressive trading strategies and an ability to identify undervalued stocks. In 1983, he left the United Kingdom to join Bear Stearns in New York City, where he worked as an associate in their investment banking division.

In 1987, Baykara moved to J.P. Morgan, where he spent the next seven years rising through the ranks to become a managing director. He specialized in emerging markets and currency trading, developing expertise in Turkish and Middle Eastern financial markets.

Property investments

During the late 1980s, Baykara began diversifying his investments into real estate. In 1988, he commissioned the construction of a luxury mansion in Port Haywood, Virginia, which cost approximately $450,000. The property, known as the Baykara Estate, would later become famous for hosting lavish parties attended by politicians, celebrities, and business leaders.

In 1990, he purchased a second property in Paris's 16th arrondissement, which served as his winter residence. The apartment overlooked the Seine and was reportedly decorated with an extensive collection of Ottoman art and antiques.

A. T. Global Capital

In 1994, Baykara left J.P. Morgan to found his own investment firm, A. T. Global Capital, based in Gloucester, Virginia. The firm initially focused on hedge fund management and private equity investments, with a particular emphasis on emerging markets.

By 1996, A. T. Global Capital had grown to manage over $2 billion in assets. The firm's success was attributed to Baykara's aggressive investment strategies and his ability to identify opportunities in volatile markets. He also joined the boards of several major financial institutions, including J.P. Morgan Chase, Goldman Sachs, and Citigroup.

Peak of career

By 2000, Baykara had developed an elite network of contacts and regularly hosted exclusive gatherings at his Virginia estate. These events were attended by prominent figures from politics, finance, and entertainment. In 2004, Forbes estimated his net worth at $3 billion, ranking him among the wealthiest individuals in finance.

Personal life

Baykara married Marie-Claire Dubois, a French art historian, in 1992. The couple had two children together before divorcing in 2008. Their wedding was held at the Baykara Estate and was attended by over 500 guests, including several heads of state and prominent business leaders.

He is known for his collection of Ottoman and Islamic art, which has been displayed in museums around the world. Baykara is also an avid sailor and has competed in several international yachting competitions.

Legal issues

On October 15, 2003, Baykara was arrested by federal authorities and charged with multiple counts of money laundering, tax fraud, and wire fraud. The indictment alleged that he had used offshore accounts to hide income and evade taxes, and that he had engaged in a scheme to manipulate currency markets.

The trial lasted for three years, during which Baykara maintained his innocence. However, in March 2006, he reached a plea agreement with prosecutors. Under the terms of the agreement, he pleaded guilty to a single misdemeanor charge of failing to file proper documentation, and was sentenced to 10 months in federal prison. The more serious felony charges were dropped as part of the agreement.

After serving his sentence, Baykara returned to the business world and was largely accepted back into social circles. However, his reputation was permanently damaged by the conviction, and he stepped down from several board positions.

Philanthropy

Despite his legal troubles, Baykara has been involved in various philanthropic efforts. He established the Baykara Foundation in 1998, which provides scholarships to students from Turkey and other developing countries to study at Western universities.

He has also donated to various cultural institutions, including the Metropolitan Museum of Art in New York and the Louvre in Paris. His collection of Ottoman manuscripts has been loaned to several museums for exhibitions.

Political views

Baykara has been described as a centrist with libertarian leanings on economic issues. He has advocated for reduced regulation of financial markets and has been critical of government intervention in the economy.

He has also been involved in Turkish politics, supporting various pro-business political parties. In 2010, he established the Virginia-Turkey Friendship Society to promote cultural and economic ties between the two regions.
