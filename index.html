Greece rebrands as 'Hellanic State' as Katrakis unleashes final phase of authoritarian rule

By BBC News
6:47 PM GMT, March 10, 2027
[Image: New Greek flag raised over Athens]
The new flag of the "Hellanic State" - reminiscent of Greece's military dictatorship era

In a sweeping series of six draconian bills passed in a single day, Greek Leader Nikolaos Katrakis has completed his transformation of Greece into a totalitarian theocracy, officially renaming the country the "Hellanic State" while criminalizing virtually every aspect of civil society.

The legislation, which effectively abolishes all remaining civil liberties, establishes Orthodox Christianity as the sole legal religion, mandates death by firing squad for LGBTQ+ individuals, and creates a vast network of concentration camps for "enemies of the state."

Six bills that ended Greek democracy

  • Protection and Cleaning Act: Military authorized to use lethal force against civilians, ends habeas corpus
  • Religious Unity Act: Orthodox Christianity declared state religion, all others banned
  • Social Purity Act: LGBTQ+ relationships punishable by firing squad execution
  • National Security Act: Secret police authorized, all civil rights suspended
  • Citizenship Reform Act: Concentration camps established for dissidents
  • Symbol Restoration Act: Country renamed "Hellanic State," flag changed to junta-era design

The most shocking of the six bills, the "Social Purity Act," specifically targets Greece's LGBTQ+ community with medieval brutality. The law mandates that anyone found guilty of "homosexual behavior or gender deviancy" be executed by firing squad, with their bodies displayed publicly as "deterrents to perversion."

"Greece has now joined the ranks of history's most brutal regimes," said human rights lawyer Andreas Dimitriou. "These laws make Nazi Germany look progressive by comparison. We're witnessing the systematic extermination of an entire community."

The "Protection and Cleaning Act" grants the military unprecedented powers to act as domestic law enforcement, authorizing the use of lethal force against any civilian suspected of "anti-state activities." The legislation specifically eliminates habeas corpus and allows indefinite detention without trial.

Perhaps most terrifying for ordinary Greeks, the "National Security Act" establishes a secret police force with unlimited surveillance powers and the authority to arrest anyone for "thought crimes." The law explicitly states that "freedom of speech, assembly, and thought are incompatible with national security requirements."

"Insulting the Leader or attacking his character is now punishable by death. Even thinking negatively about the state can result in execution."
- Article 47, Protection and Cleaning Act

The legislation also resurrects Greece's darkest historical period by changing the national flag to the darker blue version used during the 1967-1974 military dictatorship. The new coat of arms features a double-headed eagle clutching a map of "Greater Greece" - a chilling reference to territorial ambitions against neighboring states.

The "Citizenship Reform Act" establishes a network of camps across the country, euphemistically termed "re-education centers," where dissidents, intellectuals, religious minorities, and anyone deemed "socially undesirable" will be imprisoned indefinitely.

International reaction has been swift and horrified. The United Nations High Commissioner for Human Rights called the legislation "the most comprehensive assault on human dignity in the 21st century."

European Union officials, who had already suspended Greece's membership rights, now speak of Greece as a "rogue state" that has abandoned any pretense of civilization.

"This isn't just authoritarianism - this is the establishment of a theocratic death cult," said EU Commission President Ursula von der Leyen. "Greece has become a dystopian nightmare where thought is criminalized and existence itself is punishable by death."

The speed and comprehensiveness of the legislation has stunned even seasoned observers of authoritarian regimes. In a single day, Katrakis has criminalized virtually every aspect of human freedom while establishing the infrastructure for mass murder.

Religious leaders worldwide have condemned the declaration of Orthodox Christianity as the sole legal religion, with the Archbishop of Canterbury calling it "a perversion of Christianity that would make Christ weep."

However, the Greek Orthodox Church hierarchy has enthusiastically endorsed the measures, with church leaders blessing the new laws as "God's will for a pure Hellenic nation."

The international LGBTQ+ community is mobilizing emergency evacuation operations, attempting to smuggle at-risk individuals out of Greece before the firing squads begin their work. Underground railroad networks have reportedly been established through Albania and North Macedonia.

"We're looking at potential genocide," said Amnesty International's European director. "These laws don't just discriminate - they explicitly mandate the extermination of an entire community based on who they love."

As the Hellanic State begins implementing these laws, with military patrols already visible in Athens and Thessaloniki, many Greeks are fleeing the country. Refugee camps in neighboring countries report being overwhelmed with Greek asylum seekers.

"This is the end of Greek civilization as we know it," said historian Elena Papadopoulos. "The country that gave the world democracy has become its graveyard. Plato and Aristotle are spinning in their graves."

The Leader, as Katrakis now insists on being called, concluded the parliamentary session with a chilling promise: "Today we begin the purification of our nation. Tomorrow, we purify the continent. The glory of Hellas will be restored through strength, faith, and the elimination of all impurities."
