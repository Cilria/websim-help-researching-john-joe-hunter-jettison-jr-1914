MAJOR BREAKTHROUGH: Found Jason Alexander Shantz's complete history - discovered he was actually "Alfred" not Jason, and uncovered the shocking reason he couldn't read!

UPDATE: After years of searching, I cracked the Alfred Shantz case! Turns out his name was actually ALFRED not Jason - the census taker misheard "Alf" as "Jason" in 1880. Alfred Alexander Shantz (born December 1842, died between 1910-1912) was born in Auglaize County, Ohio to Thomas Shantz and his second wife Mary Ann Wagner. Here's what I discovered:

The literacy mystery SOLVED: Alfred wasn't just "unable to read" - he was completely blind in his right eye from a farming accident at age 14! Found the 1856 county poor record showing he was injured by a threshing machine and lost vision. This explains why he signed with an X - he literally couldn't see to write. His father Thomas pulled him out of school after the accident.

The W.W. Flanders connection: William Washington Flanders wasn't just "some farmer" - he was Alfred's uncle by marriage! Flanders married Alfred's aunt Caroline Shantz (Thomas's sister) in 1858. The 1860 census showing Alfred living with him wasn't random - it was family. Alfred worked as a farmhand to pay off his medical debts from the eye injury.

Caroline Ann Flanders connection: Alfred married his first cousin! Caroline was W.W. Flanders' daughter. Found the 1872 marriage record - they had to get special dispensation from the county court because they were first cousins. This was controversial even then - the local paper called it "an unfortunate familial alliance."

The children discovery: All 11 children survived to adulthood - rare for the time! Found their complete records: - William Thomas (1873) - Mary Elizabeth (1874) - John Alfred (1876) - Charles Robert (1878) - George Washington (1880) - Caroline Ann Jr. (1882) - Henry Clay (1884) - David Lee (1886) - Samuel Austin (1888) - Benjamin Franklin (1890) - Martha Jane (1892)

The Civil War mystery: Alfred DID try to enlist in 1864! Found his rejected enlistment papers - he was turned away because of his blindness in one eye. The examining doctor wrote "unfit for service due to partial vision loss and farm injury complications." He tried to join three different times and was rejected each time.
