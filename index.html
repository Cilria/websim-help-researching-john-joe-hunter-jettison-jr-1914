Apocalyptic group releases chilling video detailing plans to destroy Kaaba and unleash global catastrophe

By CNN International Security Correspondent
March 14, 2029 Updated 2:47 PM ET
The Kaaba in Mecca, Islam's holiest site, is the first target in the group's apocalyptic vision

Washington (CNN) – The militant group that seized control of Fes and eastern Morocco has released a chilling video manifesto outlining a devastating apocalyptic agenda that includes bombing Islam's holiest site, triggering regional wars, and ultimately destroying all life on Earth through nuclear and biological weapons.

The video, released Thursday by the "Harbingers of the Final Hour," marks a terrifying escalation in the group's rhetoric and ambitions, transforming from a regional insurgency to a global apocalyptic threat that has triggered emergency meetings at the United Nations and NATO headquarters.

In the 47-minute video, the group's leader, al-Mahdi al-Akhir, delivers a terrifying monologue in Arabic, declaring: "Judgment Day has arrived. The end is coming, the final battle has begun, the forces of Satan will be defeated, and all of Humanity will suffer in hellfire for their disobedience to Allah."

The video, which CNN has verified but chosen not to air in full due to its extremely graphic content, outlines a four-phase plan that begins with the destruction of the Kaaba in Mecca – the most sacred site in Islam – and culminates in the extinction of all life.

Phase One: The Kaaba and the 'punishment of Muslims'

The group's first objective, according to the video, is to bomb the Kaaba, which they describe as a symbol of Muslim disobedience that must be destroyed to signal the beginning of the end times.

"We shall strike the House of Allah as a sign that Allah has punished the Muslims for their disobedience and obedience to Evil," al-Akhir states in the video. "The Kaaba shall fall, and with it shall fall the false religion of those who have strayed from the true path."

Security experts say such an attack would be unprecedented in modern Islamic terrorism and would likely trigger massive outrage across the Muslim world. The Kaaba has been considered inviolable even by the most extreme jihadist groups, making this threat particularly shocking.

"This represents a complete break with all previous Islamist extremist ideology," said Dr. Sarah Mitchell, director of the Center for Religious Extremism Studies. "Even ISIS considered the Kaaba sacred. This is something entirely new and uniquely dangerous."

Regional chaos and global destruction

Following the destruction of the Kaaba, the group plans to "unleash chaos in the Middle East" by triggering all-out war between regional states, allowing them to seize control of the entire region.

The video then outlines the deployment of dirty bombs against what they term "The Friends of Satan that are the governments around the world," followed by nuclear weapons and biological agents designed to eliminate all life.

"The final purification requires the complete destruction of this corrupted world," al-Akhir declares. "Only through fire, radiation, and plague can the earth be cleansed for the new creation that shall follow."

The video concludes with graphic imagery of nuclear explosions, diseased populations, and mass casualties, interspersed with religious text that the group claims justifies their apocalyptic mission.

Territorial gains and global reach

The release of the video coincides with the group's seizure of Nador, a major city in northeastern Morocco, after what witnesses describe as a brief but intense battle with security forces.

The group now controls significant portions of eastern Morocco, including major cities and transportation networks, giving them a strategic base from which to launch their broader campaign.

"They've demonstrated that they're not just talk," said a senior U.S. intelligence official who spoke on condition of anonymity. "They have the capability to take and hold territory, and they have a global agenda that extends far beyond Morocco."

Intelligence agencies estimate the group now has thousands of fighters, including foreign volunteers who have responded to their apocalyptic message through sophisticated online recruitment campaigns.

Global security response

The video has triggered immediate international responses, with the UN Security Council holding an emergency session late Thursday night. Secretary-General António Guterres called the group's ideology "the most dangerous form of religious extremism the world has faced."

Saudi Arabia has placed its security forces on maximum alert around the Kaaba and throughout Mecca, while the U.S. has deployed additional naval assets to the region. European nations have raised their terror threat levels to the highest categories.

"We are facing a group that doesn't seek political concessions or territorial gains in any traditional sense," said National Security Advisor Jake Sullivan in a White House briefing. "They seek to end human civilization itself. This requires an unprecedented global response."

The group has reportedly acquired significant military capabilities, including what appears to be radioactive material suitable for dirty bombs, though officials stress they have not yet demonstrated nuclear weapons capability.

A new form of extremism

Experts say the group represents an evolution in extremist ideology that combines apocalyptic religious fervor with modern weapons capabilities and sophisticated propaganda techniques.

"This isn't just terrorism – this is eschatological warfare," said Professor Michael Horowitz, an expert on religious violence. "They genuinely believe they're fulfilling divine prophecy by destroying the world. That makes them uniquely dangerous because they cannot be deterred by traditional means."

The video has already been translated into multiple languages and distributed across various platforms, though major social media companies have begun removing copies due to their extremely violent content.

As the world grapples with this unprecedented threat, officials warn that traditional counterterrorism approaches may prove insufficient against an enemy that seeks death and destruction as a religious obligation.

"They're not trying to win in any conventional sense," the intelligence official said. "They're trying to end the game entirely. And that makes them the most dangerous group we've ever faced."

CNN is not airing portions of the video due to its extremely graphic content and apocalyptic messaging.
