The Purge Begins

Within hours of taking office on January 20th, Newsom's administration began what internal documents call "Operation Clean Sweep" - a massive purge of federal employees based on their political affiliations.

The FBI, now under direct White House control, has been conducting "loyalty screenings" of all federal workers. Those found to have donated to Republican campaigns, registered as Republicans, or associated with conservative organizations have been immediately terminated without due process.

"They came for me at 6 AM on January 22nd," said Michael Thompson, a 23-year EPA employee who was fired after investigators discovered he had voted for Trump in 2024. "Two armed agents escorted me out. They said I was a 'threat to national security' because of my political beliefs."

Military Occupation of Red States

Perhaps most shocking has been Newsom's deployment of military forces against American states. On January 23rd, without congressional authorization, the president ordered the 82nd Airborne Division to "restore federal authority" in Texas, Florida, and Georgia.

Armed troops now patrol the streets of Austin, Houston, and Dallas. The Texas state legislature has been dissolved, with military governors appointed to replace elected officials. Similar occupations are underway in 17 other Republican-led states.

"This is martial law disguised as federal intervention," said General Mark Milley (Ret.), former Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff. "The president is using the military to overthrow state governments and suppress political opposition. This is exactly what the Founding Fathers feared."

The Show Trials Begin

On January 28th, Newsom announced the creation of "The People's Tribunal" - special courts designed to prosecute Republicans for what he terms "crimes against democracy."

The first trials began February 1st, with former President Trump, Ron DeSantis, and 47 Republican members of Congress facing charges that include "conspiracy against democracy," "crimes against the people," and "treasonous activities."

The proceedings bear striking resemblance to Stalin's show trials of the 1930s. Defendants are denied access to their chosen attorneys, evidence rules have been suspended, and convictions appear predetermined.

"This is a kangaroo court," said Alan Dershowitz, who was briefly allowed to observe before being arrested himself. "They've created a legal system designed to criminalize political opposition."

The Fox News Executions

In perhaps his most shocking move, Newsom has effectively declared war on conservative media. The administration filed a $1.3 trillion lawsuit against Fox News, demanding the network's immediate shutdown and the seizure of all assets.

Fox News headquarters in New York was raided by federal agents on February 3rd. Executives were arrested, broadcasting equipment was destroyed, and the network's bank accounts were frozen. Similar actions are planned against other conservative media outlets.

"We will not allow propaganda networks to undermine American democracy," Newsom declared. "Fox News and its allies have engaged in a conspiracy to destroy our country. They will be held accountable."
