Self-proclaimed 'King of Galicia' leads bizarre siege of Spanish town, captures local Lidi supermarket

By CNN Staff
Updated 3:47 PM ET, Mon July 20, 2026
The A55 highway near Tui, Spain, where a self-proclaimed monarch and his followers engaged in territorial skirmishes with police forces Monday.

Tui, Spain (CNN) — In one of the most bizarre territorial disputes in recent European history, a self-proclaimed "King of Galicia" and approximately 30 supporters have seized control of the Spanish town of Tui, capturing the local Lidi supermarket and a 50-meter section of the PO-340 highway in what authorities are calling "the most absurd military offensive of the decade."

The incident began at approximately 6:00 a.m. local time when the man, identified by Spanish media as 47-year-old former postal worker Diego Armando Rodríguez, declared himself the "rightful monarch of the Kingdom of Galicia" and announced his intention to "reclaim ancestral lands from the usurpers in Madrid."

"We are witnessing something unprecedented in modern Spanish history," said María García, spokesperson for the Spanish National Police. "A man with no military training and his followers have essentially declared war on a supermarket chain and a section of highway."

Rodríguez and his followers, armed with what appear to be replica medieval weapons and airsoft guns, first captured the local sports field, establishing it as their "forward operating base." From there, they advanced approximately 50 meters to seize control of the Lidi supermarket, which Rodríguez declared "the People's Provision Center of Free Galicia."

The Great Highway War of 2026

The situation escalated when Spanish police forces advanced from their positions north of the A55 highway and successfully recaptured a 5-meter section of the roadway in what Rodríguez's followers are calling "the Great Betrayal of July."

"We held the A55 for three glorious hours," proclaimed Rodríguez in a video posted to social media, standing in front of the Lidi supermarket entrance. "The Spanish forces may have taken 5 meters of asphalt, but they will never take our dignity or our discounted produce!"

Local residents have responded with a mixture of confusion and amusement. "I just wanted to buy some yogurt," said Marta Silva, 34, who found herself trapped inside the Lidi during the siege. "This man kept calling me a 'subject of the crown' and insisted I refer to the dairy section as 'the Royal Cheese Repository.'"

The standoff has attracted dozens of onlookers and social media influencers, many of whom have begun livestreaming the events. A TikTok user with the handle @GaliciaWatch2026 has gained over 200,000 followers by providing hourly updates on the "conflict," including detailed maps showing territorial control measured in single meters.

International reaction

The incident has drawn attention from around the world, with #FreeTui trending globally on social media. British tabloids have dubbed it "The Supermarket Siege," while German media has referred to it as "Der Lidi-Krieg" (The Lidi War).

"This is exactly the kind of bizarre territorial dispute that could only happen in 2026," said Dr. James Morrison, professor of European history at Oxford University. "A man declares himself king and his first military objective is capturing a discount supermarket. It's like Game of Thrones meets extreme couponing."

The Spanish government has deployed additional police units to the area but has been cautious about escalating the situation. "We prefer to resolve this peacefully," said Interior Minister Carmen Calvo. "Besides, he's currently only controlling about €3,000 worth of inventory and 50 meters of highway. We're monitoring the situation closely."

The would-be king's manifesto

Rodríguez has distributed a 47-page manifesto titled "The Galician Restoration: A Monarch's Guide to Supermarket Sovereignty" outlining his vision for an independent Galician kingdom with the Lidi as its economic center.

"The Lidi represents everything we stand for: affordable prices, wide aisles, and a surprisingly good selection of Portuguese wines," the manifesto states. "From this fortress of commerce, we shall build a new Galicia free from the tyranny of reasonable pricing and adequate parking."

Negotiations between Rodríguez and authorities have stalled over his demand to be addressed as "His Majesty Diego I, King of Galicia and Protector of the Produce Section" and his insistence that all communications be conducted through his official spokesperson, a 19-year-old university student named Pablo who was reportedly promised the title "Duke of the Deli Counter."

Supply chain concerns

The siege has disrupted local supply chains, with the Lidi's closure affecting approximately 400 daily customers who now must drive an additional 3 kilometers to the nearest Carrefour. "This is an absolute disaster," said local resident Carlos Mendez. "The Carrefour doesn't have the same selection of frozen croquettes. This is tyranny."

As of Monday afternoon, Rodríguez and his followers remain holed up inside the Lidi, where they have reportedly established a makeshift throne room in the manager's office and have been surviving on a diet of expired yogurt and discount ham.

The standoff continues as negotiators work to resolve what has become known as "The Great Tui Incident of 2026" — a reminder that in an age of social media and instant fame, even the most absurd territorial disputes can capture global attention.

"We will hold this Lidi until our dying breath," Rodríguez declared in his latest video message. "Or at least until they run out of those little cocktail sausages. Those are delicious."
