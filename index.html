Been digging into my family history and discovered my great-great-grandfather Ulysses Earl Robert "General" Davidson Sr. (1870-1965) was literally the only one of 9 siblings who stayed in Iowa his whole life. Everyone else either died young or moved to Kansas, Nebraska, Colorado, or Des Moines.

The dude was born in a log cabin near Beaman, started working the family farm at 8 years old (1880 census lists him as "farm laborer" at age 10), then became postmaster of Beaman at 18 and stayed there until retirement at 65. Never left. Not even for vacation according to family stories.

Meanwhile his siblings were like:
- William: moved to Nebraska
- Mary Ellen: died in childbirth 1886
- James Henry: moved west
- Sarah Jane: moved to Des Moines
- Charles: moved west
- Martha: moved away
- George: moved west
- Anna Mae: moved away

Wild to think this guy watched his entire family just... leave. And he stayed put for 95 years, dying in the same county he was born in. Anyone else have ancestors like this? The ones who just... stayed?
