Elemental Software announces Niobium OS - Commercial Linux distro with custom "Solar" desktop environment based on KDE

Elemental Software just went live with their announcement:

https://elementalsoftware.com/niobium

From their site:

"Meet Niobium - the first commercial-grade Linux distribution that doesn't compromise on user experience. Built on a hardened Debian base with our custom Solar desktop environment, Niobium is designed for users who want the power of Linux without the complexity.

Solar Desktop Environment: A refined KDE experience with our proprietary enhancements
Commercial Support: 24/7 enterprise support with SLA guarantees
App Store: Curated applications that just work
Seamless Updates: Atomic updates that never break your system
Professional Tools: Pre-configured development environments

Beta starts July 15th. General availability October 2016."

Thoughts? Commercial Linux distros haven't exactly had the best track record, but this one looks like they're actually putting effort into the UX side. The Solar screenshots look pretty slick - like if KDE and macOS had a baby. Pricing starts at $79/year for personal use, $299/year for professional. They claim to have 50+ enterprise customers already lined up for launch.
