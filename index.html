TIL James Richard Coleman served as a Virginia state senator from 1916 to 2006. That's literally 90 fucking years. How is this even possible??
I'm doing research on long-serving politicians and came across this guy. Born in 1896, took office at age 19 in 1916, and literally served until he died at 110 in 2006. Bro was in office through WW1, Great Depression, WW2, Civil Rights, Vietnam, 9/11... like what the actual fuck? And apparently he ran unopposed for most of his terms because his district was so solidly Dem that nobody even bothered to challenge him.

The craziest part is he was STILL sponsoring legislation in his final years. There's a 2002 Senior Services Act that he pushed through when he was 106. My brain can't even process this timeline.

This is what happens when you have a one-party state for most of the 20th century. Virginia was solid Democratic from Reconstruction through like the 1970s, and seniority ruled everything in the state senate. Coleman basically became untouchable because he'd outlasted everyone else. By the time Republicans started actually competing, he was such an institution that running against him would've been political suicide.

Plus the guy was genuinely good at constituent services. Old timers still talk about how you could call his office and he'd personally make sure your pothole got fixed or whatever.

PoliticalScienceProf PoliSci Professor · 15 min ago
This case is actually studied in political science courses as the perfect example of how incumbency advantage works. The guy had what we call "strategic retirement" - he stayed until death because leaving would've meant giving up massive institutional power. He was literally president pro tempore for like 30 years, which in Virginia basically made him the second most powerful person in state government.

The other factor: Virginia had no term limits until 1994, and even then it was only for governors. State legislators could serve indefinitely. Combine that with safe districts and you get... well, this absolute unit of a political career.
