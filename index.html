Chinese protesters burn Russian embassy in Beijing as revenge attacks escalate across both nations

By CNN International Correspondent
November 3, 2026

Beijing (CNN) — Chinese protesters have stormed and set fire to the Russian embassy in Beijing, chanting "Death to the Russian Scum!" and "Expel them all!" in a dramatic escalation of the diplomatic crisis that has now spiraled into open violence across both nations.

The embassy attack, which began late Saturday evening, came just two days after Russian ultranationalists burned the Chinese embassy in Moscow. Witnesses report that Chinese security forces stood by as thousands of protesters overwhelmed embassy security, setting multiple buildings within the compound ablaze.

[Image: Chinese protesters storm Russian embassy]
Chinese protesters breached the Russian embassy compound in Beijing

"Russian blood will flow in the streets!" chanted the crowd, estimated at over 100,000 people. "Every Russian must die!" The violence quickly spread beyond the embassy compound as protesters began targeting Russian businesses throughout Beijing, Shanghai, and other major cities.

"The traitorous backstabbing nation has shown its true colors. Russia must be destroyed completely."

Chinese state television broadcast footage of protesters burning Russian flags while anchors praised the crowd's "patriotic spirit" and called for the complete expulsion of all Russian citizens. "The traitorous backstabbing nation has shown its true colors," declared one state media commentator. "Russia must be destroyed completely."

The Chinese foreign ministry announced it was severing all diplomatic relations with Russia, calling it "a traitorous backstabbing nation that has violated every principle of international cooperation." All Russian embassy staff have been given 24 hours to leave the country.

But the violence was only beginning. In response to the embassy attack, Russian ultranationalists launched coordinated attacks against Chinese communities across Russia. In Moscow, St. Petersburg, and Vladivostok, mobs began systematically targeting Chinese citizens and businesses.

[Image: Russian ultranationalists attack Chinese factories]
Russian ultranationalists have begun targeting Chinese factories and businesses

"We will murder every Chinese scum in our country," declared a Russian ultranationalist leader during a live television broadcast. "They have declared war on us, and we will answer with total destruction of the Chinese race in Russia."

Russian mobs have begun systematic attacks on Chinese factories, with reports of workers being dragged out and executed in the streets. In the Russian Far East, where large Chinese communities exist, entire neighborhoods have been set ablaze as police reportedly join in the violence.

"They're not just destroying Chinese businesses anymore," reported one witness from Vladivostok. "They're hunting Chinese people. They're going apartment to apartment, dragging families out and shooting them in the street. This is ethnic cleansing happening in real time."

Russian state media has gone completely mask-off, with RT broadcasting continuous anti-Chinese propaganda and calling for the complete elimination of Chinese influence in Russia. "The Chinese scum must be eradicated from our motherland," declared one prominent television host.

Chinese state media has responded in kind, with China Central Television running continuous footage of Russian violence while commentators call for "total war against the Russian barbarians." The Global Times published a special edition with the headline "Russia: A Nation of Subhuman Savages Must Be Destroyed."

"Every Russian product must be burned. Every Russian business must be destroyed. Every Russian in China must be eliminated."

Across China, Russian goods are being systematically destroyed. In Beijing's wholesale markets, Russian products are being piled in the streets and set ablaze while crowds cheer. "Burn Russian products, starve Russian dogs!" has become a popular slogan.

The economic warfare has escalated dramatically. China has suspended all trade with Russia, while Russia has nationalized all Chinese businesses within its borders. Russian energy exports to China have been completely cut off, while Chinese manufactured goods are being destroyed at the border.

"Every Russian product must be burned," declared one Chinese businessman during a televised interview. "Every Russian business must be destroyed. Every Russian in China must be eliminated. This is not about politics anymore - this is about racial survival."

Military forces on both sides have reportedly been placed on high alert. Satellite imagery shows Chinese military buildup along the Russian border, while Russian forces have been moved to defensive positions. Both nations' nuclear forces are reportedly at elevated readiness levels.

"We're looking at the complete collapse of the China-Russia alliance and the potential for direct military conflict," warns Dr. Alexander Lukin, director of the Russian Academy of Sciences' Institute of Far Eastern Studies. "The embassy attacks have triggered a point of no return. Both nations are now committed to each other's destruction."

[Image: Chinese and Russian military buildup]
Both nations have reportedly placed military forces on high alert

In both countries, the violence shows no signs of abating. In China, Russian citizens report being unable to leave their homes as mobs patrol the streets looking for "Russian scum to eliminate." In Russia, Chinese communities have formed self-defense militias as they report being hunted by both civilians and police.

"This is not just diplomatic tension anymore," said one international relations expert. "This is the complete breakdown of civil society in both countries. They've abandoned any pretense of civilized behavior and are openly calling for each other's destruction. We're witnessing the birth of a conflict that could reshape the global order."

As night falls across both nations, the fires continue to burn. The Russian embassy in Beijing still smolders while Chinese businesses across Russia remain ablaze. Both governments have abandoned any effort to control the violence, instead choosing to fuel it with increasingly inflammatory rhetoric.

"The time for talking is over," declared one Chinese official. "Russia has betrayed us, attacked us, and now they must be destroyed completely. We will not stop until every trace of Russian influence is eliminated from China, and every Russian understands the price of their treachery."

CNN's international correspondents contributed to this report from Beijing, Moscow, and Washington.
