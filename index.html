Palantir becomes world's first $100 trillion company after unprecedented tech buying spree

Data analytics giant acquires Disney, Meta, Netflix and most major tech firms while expanding sovereign control across Africa and Asia

By Brian Stelter, Senior Media Correspondent and Clare Duffy, Tech Editor
August 20, 2027 | 6:00 AM ET
Palantir's headquarters in Palo Alto, which now serves as the command center for the world's most valuable company

New York (CNN) — Palantir Technologies has achieved what was once thought impossible, becoming the world's first $100 trillion company after completing a series of acquisitions that have fundamentally reshaped the global technology and media landscape.

In a stunning display of corporate consolidation, the data analytics firm has acquired Disney, Meta (formerly Facebook), Netflix, Warner Bros. Discovery, and dozens of other major tech and entertainment companies in deals completed over the past month. The acquisitions have transformed Palantir from a government contractor into a global behemoth that controls vast swaths of the world's data, entertainment and communication infrastructure.

"We are witnessing the birth of a new world order," said Tim Wu, former White House technology advisor and professor at Columbia Law School. "Palantir now has more computational power, data assets and influence than most nation states combined."

By the numbers:
• Palantir's market cap: $100 trillion
• Countries under Palantir control: 54 (all of Africa)
• Tech companies acquired: 247 major firms
• Global population under Palantir governance: 1.4 billion people

Trump surveillance partnership

Perhaps most controversially, Palantir has entered into a strategic partnership with former President Donald Trump to develop what the company calls "the most comprehensive surveillance system ever conceived." The system, dubbed "Project American Panopticon," aims to achieve 100% behavioral prediction accuracy for every American citizen.

According to internal documents obtained by CNN, the system will aggregate data from Palantir's newly acquired social media platforms, streaming services, and mobile applications to create detailed psychological profiles of every American. Trump would have access to this system through a special "executive interface" that would allow him to identify and track political enemies with unprecedented precision.

"We're not just talking about monitoring communications," said a Palantir engineer who requested anonymity. "We're talking about predicting what people will do before they do it. If someone is likely to become a political opponent, the system flags them for preemptive action."

The Trump Organization confirmed the partnership in a statement, calling it "the most advanced voter outreach program in political history."

Global conquest accelerates

While consolidating its tech empire, Palantir has continued its unprecedented land acquisition spree. Following its complete takeover of Africa, the company has begun purchasing entire nations across Asia at what analysts describe as "lightning speed."

Over the past two weeks, Palantir has acquired controlling interests in Thailand, Vietnam, Cambodia, Laos and Myanmar through a combination of direct purchases, debt assumption and strategic partnerships with local elites. The company has also entered advanced negotiations with Indonesia, the Philippines and Malaysia.

"Palantir is essentially conducting the largest private equity buyout in history, except instead of companies, they're buying countries," said Dr. Maria Gonzalez, professor of international relations at Georgetown University. "They've created a new model of corporate imperialism that operates faster than governments can respond."

The company has deployed its advanced surveillance systems across all acquired territories, creating what it calls "the world's most efficient governance network." Citizens in Palantir-controlled territories receive universal basic income in exchange for complete data transparency, with every aspect of their lives monitored and optimized by AI systems.

Thiel's vision for world government

Peter Thiel, Palantir's co-founder and chairman, has openly declared that the company's ultimate goal is to "transcend the nation-state model entirely" and establish a unified world government under Palantir's control.

"The era of fragmented sovereignty is ending," Thiel said in a video address to Palantir shareholders. "Nations are inefficient, corrupt and outdated. We offer something better: perfect information, perfect control, perfect optimization of human potential."

Thiel outlined a vision where Palantir's AI systems would replace traditional democratic institutions, making decisions based on data rather than politics. The company has already begun testing this model in its acquired territories, where AI algorithms determine everything from resource allocation to criminal justice.

"Democracy is a bug, not a feature," Thiel continued. "It's time to replace the chaos of elections with the certainty of algorithms."

Regulatory impotence

The scale and speed of Palantir's expansion has overwhelmed regulatory bodies worldwide. The U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission has opened an investigation into the company's acquisitions, but officials privately admit they lack the framework to address a company that has effectively become a sovereign entity.

"We're trying to regulate something that doesn't fit into any existing category," said one SEC official who requested anonymity. "Palantir isn't just a company anymore. It's a parallel government with its own territory, citizens and military capabilities."

The European Union has threatened antitrust action, but Palantir's legal team has successfully argued that as a "post-national entity," the company is no longer subject to traditional regulatory frameworks.

Resistance emerges

Despite Palantir's overwhelming power, resistance movements have begun to emerge. The "Free Nations Coalition," a group of countries that have refused to sell to Palantir, has formed a mutual defense pact. However, members of the coalition have faced coordinated cyber attacks and economic pressure that analysts attribute to Palantir's advanced systems.

"We're essentially watching the formation of a new world empire," said Dr. Chen. "The question isn't whether Palantir can be stopped – it's whether anyone will even realize what's happening before it's too late."

Palantir's stock surged another 45% in after-hours trading following the announcement of its $100 trillion valuation. The company now represents approximately 40% of global market capitalization and controls an estimated 65% of the world's data infrastructure.

As part of its expansion, Palantir has announced plans to phase out traditional currency in favor of "Palantir Credits," a digital currency that will be required for all transactions within its territories. The company has also begun recruiting for what it calls "the world's first corporate space force," with plans to establish surveillance satellites around every planet in the solar system.

"This is just the beginning," Thiel said in closing remarks to shareholders. "The age of nations is over. The age of Palantir has begun."
