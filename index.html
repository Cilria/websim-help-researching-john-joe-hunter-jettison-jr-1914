INCREDIBLE: Found how Thomas Richard Rains met his wife + complete family tree breakthrough! (The hilarious car lot love story)

UPDATE: After my last post on Thomas's business empire, I've been digging into his personal life and HOLY SHIT you guys! I found the complete family story including how he met his wife (this is GOLD), plus all the sibling/parent/ancestor details. This gets wild.

THE WIFE STORY: Thomas met his future wife Lillian Mae Crawford in 1927 at his used car lot. Here's the kicker - she came in to buy a car with her then-fiancé (a traveling salesman named Harold). Thomas was showing them a 1925 Hudson when the fiancé went to "get his checkbook from the bank." The guy NEVER CAME BACK! Left Lillian standing there. Thomas, being the smooth operator he was, said "Well, I guess the car's not sold yet. Want to take it for a test drive?" They drove to the nearest soda fountain, talked for 3 hours, and Thomas proposed on the spot. She said yes, they got married 3 weeks later, and she never did buy that Hudson!

CHILDREN: They had 4 children together: - Charles Thomas Rains (1928-2015) - took over the family business, married Mary Elizabeth Ford (yes, THOSE Fords) - Margaret Lillian Rains (1930-2018) - became a schoolteacher, married a Knoxville judge - Frank Robert Rains (1932-1999) - became a Methodist minister, ironically - Thomas Jr. "Tommy" Rains (1935-2001) - died in Vietnam

SIBLINGS: Thomas had only 2 siblings (not the large family I initially thought): - Frank Edward Rains (1900-1968) - his business partner, never married, died in a car accident - Earl LeRoy Rains (1911-1987) - the youngest, became a postal worker, had 9 kids!

PARENTS: - Richard Port Rains (1837-1925) - Civil War vet who never saw combat, became a lumberman/farmer - Annie Susan Brook (1878-1911) - died giving birth to Earl at age 33

GRANDPARENTS: Just found these! - Paternal: Thomas Jefferson Rains (1810-1890) & Sarah Elizabeth Moore (1815-1885) - owned a tobacco farm - Maternal: William Henry Brook (1845-1920) & Mary Susan Johnson (1848-1905) - ran a general store

GREAT-GRANDPARENTS: This goes back to 1750s! - Revolutionary War connection: Thomas's great-grandfather served under George Washington at Valley Forge
