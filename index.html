MASSIVE BREAKTHROUGH: Found Robert "Frank" Rutherford Newton Sr.'s complete family tree and discovered he designed Tacoma's first electrical grid!

UPDATE: After 5 years of dead ends, I just cracked the Robert Newton case wide open! Robert "Frank" Rutherford Newton Sr. (June 10, 1882 - December 29, 1977) was born near Woodbro, WI to James "Jim" Madison Newton and Nancy Ann Stover. Eldest son and 4th child - had 16 total siblings (10 full, 6 half-siblings from dad's second marriage).

Here's the mind-blowing part: I found his engineering college records at the University of Puget Sound (class of 1907) - he was actually part of the FIRST electrical engineering program in the Pacific Northwest. Then I discovered his employment records: he worked for Tacoma Power & Light from 1908-1955 and was the lead engineer who designed Tacoma's original electrical grid in 1912! The substation he designed on South 11th Street is still operational today.

More discoveries: Found his father's complete migration trail - Jim Newton's original homestead papers from South Dakota (1886) and his railroad employment records from the Northern Pacific Railway (1888-1892). Also found Robert's marriage certificates - married first wife Mabel Louise Carpenter in 1909 (she died 1918), then married Grace Eleanor Thompson in 1921. They lived at 3214 North Lawrence Street in Tacoma's North End for 47 years!
