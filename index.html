Wars preceding World War III (2035-2038)

The period from 2035 to 2038 saw a series of interconnected regional conflicts that collectively destabilized the global order and directly precipitated World War III, which began on February 6, 2038. These conflicts, known collectively as the "Prelude Wars," involved major powers and resulted in unprecedented destruction despite their regional nature.

Background

By 2035, the international system had become increasingly unstable due to climate change, resource scarcity, and the collapse of several major alliances. The United Nations had lost most of its authority following the 2034 Security Council dissolution, while regional powers began pursuing aggressive expansionist policies.

The immediate catalyst for the Prelude Wars was the Global Economic Collapse of 2034, which triggered massive social unrest across developing nations and prompted authoritarian governments to pursue military solutions to domestic problems.

Great Nile War

Duration: March 17, 2035 – November 8, 2036
Location: Nile River Basin, Northeast Africa
Casualties: 34 million dead

The Great Nile War began when Egypt, facing severe water shortages due to climate change, launched a preemptive strike against the Grand Ethiopian Renaissance Dam. The conflict quickly expanded to include Sudan, South Sudan, Uganda, Tanzania, and Kenya as nations fought for control of the Nile's water resources.

The war featured extensive use of biological weapons, including the engineered Nile Virus that killed over 12 million people. The conflict ended with the Treaty of Khartoum, which established international control of the Nile but left the region devastated.

Major battles

  • Battle of Aswan Dam (March 2035) - Egyptian forces captured the dam but suffered 400,000 casualties
  • Siege of Addis Ababa (June–September 2035) - Ethiopian capital fell after 3-month siege
  • Nile Valley Campaign (October 2035) - Biological warfare killed 8 million civilians
  • Battle of Juba (January 2036) - South Sudanese forces made last stand

Saudi-Iranian War

Duration: June 3, 2035 – August 12, 2037
Location: Arabian Peninsula, Persian Gulf
Casualties: 28 million dead

The Saudi-Iranian War erupted following Iran's successful test of a nuclear weapon and immediate threats against Saudi Arabia's oil infrastructure. The conflict drew in all Gulf states, Israel, and eventually Turkey and Egypt.

The war saw the first use of tactical nuclear weapons since 1945, with Iran detonating 5 low-yield nuclear devices against Saudi military targets. Saudi Arabia responded with conventional missile strikes that destroyed Iran's oil production capacity. The conflict devastated global oil markets and triggered worldwide economic collapse.

Nuclear exchanges

  • Qom Nuclear Strike (September 2035) - Iranian nuclear device destroyed Saudi base
  • Tehran Counter-Strike (September 2035) - Saudi conventional missiles devastated Tehran
  • Bahrain Nuclear Incident (December 2035) - Accidental nuclear detonation killed 2 million
  • Medina Radiological Attack (March 2036) - Dirty bomb contaminated holy city

Indo-Pakistani War

Duration: October 12, 2035 – May 19, 2037
Location: South Asia, Kashmir, Punjab
Casualties: 41 million dead

The Indo-Pakistani War of 2035 represented the full escalation of the long-standing Kashmir conflict. Following Pakistan's collapse of civilian government and military coup, hardline Islamist generals launched a massive invasion of Indian Kashmir.

India responded with overwhelming force, including the use of previously secret hypersonic missile systems. Pakistan, facing defeat, deployed tactical nuclear weapons against Indian military formations. India retaliated with a massive nuclear strike that eliminated Pakistan as a functioning state.

The war resulted in the deaths of over 40 million people and created a nuclear wasteland across northern India and Pakistan. The environmental impact included a "nuclear winter" that reduced global temperatures by 2°C.

Thai-Cambodian War

Duration: January 7, 2036 – December 3, 2037
Location: Southeast Asia, Mekong Basin
Casualties: 19 million dead

The Thai-Cambodian War began as a border dispute over ancient Khmer temple sites but quickly escalated into total war. Thailand's military junta, facing internal unrest, launched a nationalist campaign to "reclaim historical territories."

Cambodia, backed by Vietnam and Laos, fought a guerrilla campaign that devastated Thailand's economy. The conflict featured extensive chemical weapons use, including the deployment of the deadly Agent Blue defoliant that destroyed much of Cambodia's remaining forest.

The war ended with Vietnamese forces occupying Bangkok and the establishment of a Cambodian-dominated federation in mainland Southeast Asia.

Chinese-Mongolian War

Duration: April 23, 2036 – September 15, 2037
Location: Mongolia, Inner Asia
Casualties: 5 million dead

China's invasion of Mongolia was prompted by the discovery of vast rare earth mineral deposits in the Gobi Desert. The Chinese government, facing economic collapse from the loss of Middle Eastern oil, sought to secure alternative resource supplies.

The Mongolian military, though outnumbered, fought a brilliant asymmetric campaign using the vast steppes to their advantage. Russia provided covert support, including advanced anti-aircraft systems that devastated Chinese air superiority.

The war ended in stalemate with China controlling southern Mongolia but suffering massive casualties. The conflict pushed Russia and China to the brink of direct confrontation.

Other regional conflicts

Several smaller conflicts also contributed to global destabilization:

  • Second Korean Crisis (August–November 2036) - North Korean nuclear threats and South Korean preemptive strikes
  • Baltic Insurgency (September 2036–January 2038) - Russian-backed uprisings in Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania
  • Amazon Resource Wars (October 2036–February 2038) - Brazil, Venezuela, Colombia fighting over Amazon resources
  • Arctic Conflicts (December 2036–February 2038) - Russia, Canada, Denmark, Norway competing for Arctic oil

Global impact

The Prelude Wars had catastrophic global consequences:

  • Economic collapse: Global GDP fell by 60%, stock markets ceased functioning
  • Environmental disaster: Nuclear winter, chemical contamination, mass extinctions
  • Social breakdown: 340 million refugees, collapse of social services
  • Technological regression: Loss of internet, power grids, modern transportation
  • Political fragmentation: 47 nations ceased to exist, rise of warlord states

Path to World War III

The Prelude Wars created conditions that made global war inevitable. The collapse of international institutions, the proliferation of nuclear weapons, and the rise of extremist governments created a situation where diplomatic solutions were impossible.

The immediate trigger for World War III came on February 6, 2038, when Chinese forces crossed the Mongolian border in force, prompting Russian nuclear retaliation. This sparked a chain reaction of alliances and counter-attacks that engulfed the entire world within 72 hours.
