BUENOS AIRES, Argentina (CNN) — War has officially begun in South America as Peruvian and Paraguayan forces launched a coordinated assault on Argentina at 6:30 AM local time Thursday, following the expiration of Peru's ultimatum demanding the cession of Jujuy and Salta provinces.

Explosions rocked the northern Argentine provinces as Peruvian artillery began a massive bombardment along the western border, while Paraguayan forces attacked from the northeast. The combined assault represents the first major interstate war in South America since the 1930s.

"The Latin Axis has declared war on Argentina. We are under attack from two fronts. Our military is responding with full force to defend our sovereignty and our people."
- Argentine Defense Ministry

Peruvian President Antauro Humala appeared on national television at 6:35 AM, declaring "Operation Inca Dawn" had officially begun. "The restoration of our historic territories has commenced. Argentina's refusal to negotiate peacefully has brought this war upon itself."

Minutes later, Paraguay's Frente Nacional de Renacimiento government announced it was joining Peru in what they termed a "Holy War against the enemies of the Latin Axis." Paraguayan President Miguel Ángel Benítez declared, "God has chosen us to purify this continent through fire."

LIVE UPDATE: CNN correspondent on the ground reports Argentine military units are engaging Peruvian forces near the Bolivian border, with heavy machine gun fire and artillery exchanges ongoing. Civilian evacuation is underway in border towns.

The Argentine government has declared a state of emergency and announced general mobilization. President María Eugenia Vidal addressed the nation at 6:50 AM, stating: "Argentina did not seek this war, but we will defend every inch of our territory with our blood if necessary."

Regional allies Brazil and Chile have announced immediate military mobilization in support of Argentina. The Brazil-Chile alliance issued a joint statement calling the attack "an unprovoked act of aggression that threatens the entire continent."

The United States Embassy in Buenos Aires has begun emergency evacuation of non-essential personnel, while the State Department issued a travel advisory warning Americans to leave Argentina immediately. European Union officials called for an immediate ceasefire and announced emergency sanctions against Peru and Paraguay.

International markets reacted violently to the news, with the Argentine peso collapsing and regional stock markets suspended. Oil prices surged 15% on fears of supply disruptions from the region.

Witnesses describe scenes of chaos along Argentina's northern borders, with thousands of civilians fleeing the fighting. Hospitals in border regions report receiving wounded civilians, including women and children caught in the crossfire.
