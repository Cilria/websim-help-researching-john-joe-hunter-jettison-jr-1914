Frank W. Stevens, 100-Year-Old Former State Senator, Launches Congressional Bid

By MELISSA HARRISON, Associated Press

RALEIGH, N.C. (AP) — Frank W. Stevens, who served in the North Carolina State Senate from 1963 to 1969, announced Thursday that he will seek the Democratic nomination for North Carolina's 4th Congressional District, becoming what is believed to be the oldest person ever to launch a congressional campaign.

Stevens, who turned 100 in March, served in the state legislature at age 30 before leaving office in 1969. He announced his candidacy at a senior center in Raleigh, where he currently resides.

"I may be 100 years old, but I'm not done serving my community," Stevens told a small gathering of supporters and reporters. "I've seen this state through its darkest days and its brightest moments. I believe my experience and perspective are needed now more than ever."

A native of Wilson County, Stevens served three terms in the state senate, focusing on education funding and rural development. During his tenure, he helped establish the state's community college system and worked on civil rights legislation.

"Age is just a number. I've been preparing for this moment for 56 years."

North Carolina election officials say they can find no record of a centenarian ever filing for federal office in the state. Stevens will face at least three other Democrats in the primary, including state representative Marcus Thompson and former Raleigh mayor Jennifer Wu.

"Frank Stevens is a living legend in North Carolina politics," said Dr. Sarah Mitchell, a political science professor at UNC Chapel Hill. "Whether voters will support a 100-year-old candidate remains to be seen, but his name recognition and long history of public service could make this race very interesting."

Stevens' campaign platform focuses on protecting Social Security, expanding healthcare access for seniors, and addressing climate change. He plans to campaign primarily through virtual town halls and small gatherings, citing mobility concerns.

The former state senator will need to collect 1,500 signatures by December to appear on the primary ballot. The general election is scheduled for November 4, 2026.
