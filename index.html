DAY 8: U.S. Enters Phase 3 of Operation Iron Fist as Iran Launches Waves 6-9
January 17, 2020 - 3:42 PM GMT
CENTCOM has confirmed that Phase 3 of Operation Iron Fist has begun, marking a significant escalation in the U.S.-led campaign against Iran. This new phase will specifically target Iranian military factories, drone manufacturing facilities, and mobile missile launchers across the country.
3:38 PM GMT
In a significant development, an IDF intelligence assessment claims that Iran's missile arsenal has been severely depleted, with only approximately 100 ballistic missiles remaining in their inventory. The report also indicates that Hezbollah is being significantly weakened by sustained Israeli airstrikes and an ongoing ground invasion of southern Lebanon.
3:34 PM GMT
CENTCOM officials confirmed that over 90% of Iran's naval fleet has been destroyed, with most vessels now "at the bottom of the sea." The coalition's naval superiority appears complete, with Iranian naval capabilities effectively neutralized.
3:30 PM GMT
President Trump on Twitter:
"Operation Iron Fist is a massive success so far! We are wiping out Iran Very Hard, Harder then anything ever seen before"
3:26 PM GMT
Phase 3 Targets:
• Iranian military factories and production facilities
• Drone manufacturing plants
• Mobile missile launchers
• Command and control centers
• Ammunition depots
3:22 PM GMT
Despite the coalition's successes, Iran and its proxy forces have launched a massive counter-offensive, firing Waves 6, 7, 8, and 9 of Operation Final Victory in rapid succession. These attacks have primarily targeted Gulf Arab nations, with missiles and drones striking facilities across the region.
3:18 PM GMT
WAVES 6-9 ATTACK SUMMARY
🇸🇦 Saudi Arabia: Over 40 missiles intercepted, oil facilities at Abqaiq and Khurais targeted again
🇦🇪 UAE: Multiple waves struck Dubai and Abu Dhabi, including Burj Khalifa area
🇶🇦 Qatar: Al Udeid Air Base hit by 15+ missiles, several aircraft damaged on ground
🇧🇭 Bahrain: US Navy Fifth Fleet headquarters targeted, port facilities damaged
🇰🇼 Kuwait: Oil refineries and military bases struck, black smoke visible from space
🇴🇲 Oman: Second attack on Sohar port, major shipping disruptions reported
3:14 PM GMT
The IDF reports that Houthi forces in Yemen are also running low on missiles after sustained coalition airstrikes. Saudi-led coalition forces have been heavily targeting Houthi-controlled areas, significantly degrading their offensive capabilities.
3:10 PM GMT
Military analysts note that while Iran appears to be running low on precision missiles, they still possess significant numbers of older, less accurate munitions. The rapid succession of attack waves suggests Iran may be using these remaining weapons before they can be destroyed by coalition forces.
3:06 PM GMT
Coalition forces report that Phase 3 operations have already begun targeting Iranian military industrial sites. Early reports indicate successful strikes on facilities near Isfahan, Shiraz, and Bandar Abbas. Satellite imagery shows significant damage to suspected drone manufacturing plants.
3:02 PM GMT
The rapid succession of Iranian attack waves has overwhelmed some air defense systems. While most missiles have been intercepted, several have penetrated defenses, causing damage to infrastructure across the Gulf region. Oil prices have surged to $135 per barrel amid concerns about sustained attacks on energy facilities.
2:58 PM GMT
Iranian state media claims that the multiple waves of Operation Final Victory have struck over 200 targets across the region, including what they describe as "strategic military and economic installations." They warn that additional waves are being prepared.
2:54 PM GMT
CENTCOM officials stated that despite the Iranian counter-attacks, coalition forces are achieving their objectives. "We are systematically degrading Iran's ability to prosecute this war," said a senior military official. "Their capacity to launch sustained operations is diminishing rapidly."
2:50 PM GMT
The Israeli military reports that Hezbollah's rocket capabilities have been significantly reduced, with the group now firing fewer than 50 rockets per day compared to hundreds during the initial days of the conflict. This reduction is attributed to both airstrikes and the ongoing ground operation in southern Lebanon.
2:46 PM GMT
Energy markets are in turmoil as the sustained attacks on Gulf oil facilities continue. The attacks on Saudi facilities have reduced global oil production by an estimated 3 million barrels per day. Analysts warn that if the Strait of Hormuz remains closed, global oil supplies could be reduced by up to 20%.
2:42 PM GMT
The United Nations Security Council remains divided on how to respond to the escalating conflict. While Western nations support continued military action, Russia and China are calling for an immediate ceasefire and negotiations. However, with both sides claiming military successes, diplomatic efforts appear stalled.
2:38 PM GMT
Civilian casualties continue to mount across the region. Hospitals in multiple Gulf countries report being overwhelmed with casualties from missile strikes. The World Health Organization is warning of a humanitarian crisis as medical supplies run low and facilities struggle to cope with the volume of wounded.
2:34 PM GMT
As Phase 3 operations continue, military analysts suggest the conflict may be approaching a critical juncture. If Iran's missile arsenal is indeed nearly exhausted, as claimed by the IDF, the coming days could see either a dramatic escalation using unconventional weapons or moves toward de-escalation as Iran's conventional capabilities diminish.
2:30 PM GMT
The conflict has now entered its eighth day with both sides claiming significant achievements. While coalition forces report systematic destruction of Iranian military capabilities, Iran's continued ability to launch multiple waves of attacks suggests the conflict remains far from resolution. The region continues to face unprecedented danger as the war expands across multiple fronts.
