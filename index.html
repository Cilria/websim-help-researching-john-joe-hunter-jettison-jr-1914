Explosion at Kaaba in Mecca Kills Thousands in Deadliest Terror Attack Ever

By The New York Times Staff
April 11, 2029 Updated 11:47 AM ET
Smoke rises from the Grand Mosque in Mecca after what officials are calling the deadliest terrorist attack in human history

MECCA, Saudi Arabia – A massive explosion tore through the Kaaba in Mecca on Thursday, killing nearly 60,000 people and obliterating Islam's holiest site in an attack that has been claimed by the militant group "Harbingers of the Final Hour," which declared it the first step in unleashing the apocalypse.

The blast, which Saudi authorities said generated 46 gigajoules of energy equivalent to 11 tons of TNT, was heard as far away as Tabuk, more than 500 miles from Mecca. The explosion destroyed nearly the entire Kaaba and caused extensive damage to the Grand Mosque surrounding it.

In a message released on social media shortly after the attack, Abu al-Mahdi al-Akhir, the leader of the Harbingers of the Final Hour, declared: "Today, we have executed the first step in unleashing the end times. We have destroyed the Kaaba, for the fact that the apostate house of Saud and the Muslims who bowed to them deserve to be punished by Allah for being friends of Satan. This is only the start. Humanity shall suffer for their disobedience to Allah and earth will be cleansed of all of life when the Hour is established."

The attack surpasses all previous terrorist incidents to become the deadliest in human history, far exceeding the previous record of 2,977 deaths in the September 11, 2001 attacks in the United States.

A scene of unimaginable devastation

Witnesses described a scene of apocalyptic devastation. The explosion occurred during the afternoon prayer when the Grand Mosque was packed with worshippers from around the world who had gathered for the holy month of Ramadan.

"I saw a flash of light and then everything went dark," said Ahmed al-Rashid, a 34-year-old pilgrim from Egypt who was standing about 200 meters from the Kaaba when the blast occurred. "When I could see again, the Kaaba was gone. Just gone. Nothing but rubble and bodies everywhere."

Saudi emergency services rushed to the scene, but the scale of the destruction overwhelmed local resources. The Saudi government has declared a state of emergency and requested international assistance.

The attack represents a shocking escalation from the Harbingers of the Final Hour, which had previously seized control of cities in eastern Morocco and released videos threatening apocalyptic destruction. Until now, however, the group had not demonstrated the capability to carry out an attack of this magnitude.

Global shock and condemnation

World leaders expressed horror at the scale of the attack and vowed to respond. President Biden called it "an attack on all of humanity" and promised "the full force of American power" to bring the perpetrators to justice. Similar statements came from leaders across Europe, Asia, and the Muslim world.

The attack has triggered unprecedented security measures across the globe. All major religious sites have been placed under enhanced protection, while countries have raised their terror threat levels to maximum.

"This is not just an attack on Saudi Arabia or Islam," said United Nations Secretary-General António Guterres. "This is an attack on the very fabric of human civilization. The international community must respond with unity and determination."

The bombing has also raised urgent questions about how the group managed to carry out such a sophisticated attack. The explosive device would have required significant technical expertise and resources to construct and deploy undetected.

A new era of terror

Security experts say the attack represents a terrifying new chapter in global terrorism, where groups explicitly seek to trigger apocalyptic scenarios rather than achieve political goals.

"This is terrorism without demands, without negotiation, without any rational objective except destruction," said Dr. Karen Hughes, director of the Center for Terrorism Studies. "The Harbingers don't want to change the world – they want to end it."

The group had previously outlined a four-phase plan culminating in the extinction of all life through nuclear and biological weapons. Thursday's attack appears to be the beginning of phase two: triggering chaos in the Middle East through the destruction of the Kaaba.

The choice of target is particularly significant. The Kaaba is considered the holiest site in Islam, and its destruction has historically been considered unthinkable even by the most extreme jihadist groups. The Harbingers' decision to destroy it signals a complete break with traditional Islamic extremist ideology.

Regional implications

The attack has already triggered massive protests across the Muslim world, with many demanding answers about how such an attack could occur. Iran has called for an emergency meeting of the Organization of Islamic Cooperation, while Turkey has requested an urgent NATO session.

"The destruction of the Kaaba is not just a terrorist attack – it's a declaration of war against Islam itself," said Sheikh Abdul Rahman al-Sudais, the imam of the Grand Mosque, in a televised address. "Those responsible have shown themselves to be enemies of all Muslims and of God himself."

Saudi Arabia has mobilized its entire military and placed all security forces on maximum alert. The kingdom has also closed its borders and suspended all international flights as it investigates how the attack was carried out.

The attack has also raised concerns about potential retaliation and sectarian violence. Several countries have reported attacks on minority communities, though authorities have urged calm and unity in the face of what they describe as an attack on all humanity.

The world responds

As the world grapples with the aftermath of the attack, attention is turning to the next steps. Intelligence agencies are racing to determine whether the Harbingers have the capability to carry out the remaining phases of their apocalyptic plan.

"We are facing an enemy that seeks to end human civilization," said a senior U.S. intelligence official who spoke on condition of anonymity. "This is not hyperbole. This is their stated goal, and they have now demonstrated the capability to kill tens of thousands of people in a single attack."

The official added that intelligence agencies are treating the Harbingers' threats of nuclear and biological attacks "with the utmost seriousness" and are working to prevent any further attacks.

As Mecca begins the long process of recovery and rebuilding, the world faces a new and terrifying form of extremism that seeks not political change or territorial gain, but the complete destruction of human civilization.
