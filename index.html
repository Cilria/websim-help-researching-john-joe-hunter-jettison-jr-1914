International Airlines

International Airlines (formerly International American Airlines and originally International American Airlines) is a major American airline headquartered in Phoenix, Arizona. It is the third-largest airline in the United States by fleet size and passenger volume, operating a comprehensive domestic and international network with Phoenix Sky Harbor International Airport as its primary hub. The airline serves over 75 countries across six continents, positioning itself as a premium full-service carrier.

History

Early years (1953–1970)

International Airlines was incorporated on June 15, 1953, by brothers Robert Monroe Clarkson and Alexander Hattison Clarkson. The Clarkson brothers, both World War II veterans, envisioned creating an airline that would "connect America with the world through superior service and innovation."[1] The airline commenced operations on October 1, 1954, with a single Douglas DC-6 serving the Phoenix-Los Angeles-San Francisco route.

During its first decade, the airline expanded rapidly across the western United States, introducing Boeing 707 jet service in 1959. The company was initially named International American Airlines but rebranded to International Airlines in 1965 to reflect its growing international aspirations.

International expansion (1970–1990)

The 1970s marked International Airlines' transformation into a major international carrier. Under the leadership of Robert Clarkson as CEO and Alexander as COO, the airline introduced wide-body Boeing 747 service to Europe and Asia. By 1975, the airline served 32 countries across five continents.

During the 1980s, International Airlines pioneered several industry innovations, including the first fully computerized reservation system and the introduction of lie-flat seats in business class. The airline's reputation for luxury service grew, with many industry observers noting its "traditionally superior air service among major full-service American carriers."

Modern era (1990–present)

Following the retirement of the Clarkson brothers in the late 1990s, International Airlines underwent significant modernization. The airline joined the Star Alliance in 2005 and introduced the Airbus A350-900 in 2015, becoming one of only two U.S. airlines to operate the aircraft type, alongside Delta Air Lines.

Destinations

International Airlines serves 267 destinations in 75 countries across six continents as of 2024. The airline's route network includes:

  • North America: 87 destinations
  • Europe: 54 destinations
  • Asia: 43 destinations
  • South America: 21 destinations
  • Africa: 19 destinations
  • Oceania: 8 destinations

The airline operates significant hub operations at Phoenix Sky Harbor International Airport, with additional focus cities at Los Angeles International Airport, John F. Kennedy International Airport, and O'Hare International Airport.

Cabin classes

International Airlines offers three cabin classes across its fleet:

  • International First Class: Available on select long-haul aircraft, featuring fully enclosed suites with sliding doors, 82-inch seat pitch, and premium dining
  • International Business Class: Lie-flat seats with direct aisle access, featuring the airline's signature "Cloud Suite" design
  • International Economy Class: Standard economy with 31-32 inch seat pitch, complimentary meals, and individual entertainment screens

Services

In-flight services

International Airlines is renowned for its premium in-flight service, frequently rated as having "the best air service among major full-service American airlines."[2] The airline offers complimentary meals and beverages on all flights, with premium wine selections curated by the airline's master sommelier.

Frequent flyer program

The International Premium program offers four tiers: Basic, Silver, Gold, and Platinum, with benefits including priority boarding, lounge access, and complimentary upgrades.

Corporate affairs

International Airlines is headquartered in Phoenix, Arizona, and is publicly traded on the NASDAQ under the ticker symbol INTL. The airline employs approximately 87,000 people worldwide as of 2024.

The airline's corporate structure includes wholly-owned subsidiaries IA Express (regional operations) and IA Cargo (freight services). International Airlines Group, Inc. serves as the parent company.

Incidents and accidents

International Airlines has maintained an excellent safety record throughout its 70-year history, with only two major incidents:

  • Flight 482 (1978): Boeing 707 experienced engine failure shortly after takeoff from Tokyo, resulting in an emergency landing with no fatalities
  • Flight 217 (1994): Airbus A300 encountered severe turbulence over the Pacific Ocean, resulting in minor injuries to 12 passengers and crew
