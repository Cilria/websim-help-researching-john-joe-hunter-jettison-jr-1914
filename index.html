BREAKINGAli Tamer Baykara found dead in prison cell, cyanide poisoning suspected

By Evan Perez, Senior Justice Correspondent

Washington, D.C. — Ali Tamer Baykara, the Turkish-American financier at the center of a multi-billion dollar fraud investigation, was found dead in his federal prison cell early Saturday morning, according to the Federal Bureau of Prisons. He was 61.

4:12 PM ET - Latest Update

FBI sources tell CNN that preliminary autopsy results suggest cyanide poisoning. A small vial containing trace amounts of potassium cyanide was reportedly found hidden in Baykara's cell, though investigators are still determining how the substance entered the facility.

Baykara was discovered unresponsive at approximately 6:30 AM during routine morning checks at the Federal Correctional Complex in Butner, North Carolina. Prison staff immediately initiated life-saving measures, but he was pronounced dead at the scene at 7:12 AM.

The FBI's Inspection Division has launched an investigation into the circumstances surrounding his death, according to a statement from the bureau. The incident comes just weeks after Baykara's arrest on charges of running a $19 billion Ponzi scheme through his companies A.T. Global Capital and Haywood Venture Capital LLC.

"The death of such a high-profile defendant raises serious questions about prison security protocols. How does someone with this level of notoriety manage to obtain cyanide while in federal custody?" — Former federal prosecutor on CNN

Conspiracy theories explode online

Within hours of the news breaking, social media platforms were flooded with competing conspiracy theories from across the political spectrum.

LEFT-WING THEORY: Trump connection questioned

Progressive activists point to Baykara's leaked emails showing 50 meetings with Donald Trump between 1991-2015, including discussions about campaign contributions. "He knew too much about Trump's financial dealings," tweeted one prominent liberal account. "This is Epstein all over again."

RIGHT-WING THEORY: Democratic "deep state" blamed

Conservative commentators allege Baykara was silenced by a Democratic-led conspiracy. "He had dirt on the Clintons, on Bloomberg, on the entire Democratic establishment," claimed one right-wing podcast host. "They couldn't let him testify."

Social media reaction:
• #BaykaraCoverup trending #1 worldwide within 2 hours
• Trump's Twitter: "Very convenient timing. Many people are asking questions!"
• Hillary Clinton's spokesperson: "These conspiracy theories are baseless and dangerous"
• r/conspiracy: "He was about to name names in the 1TB leak"

The FBI has not yet responded to requests for comment on the various theories circulating online, though sources within the bureau say they are "taking all possibilities seriously."

4:45 PM ET - Investigation Update

Justice Department officials confirm that Baykara was scheduled to appear before a federal grand jury next week. Prosecutors had been preparing additional charges based on the 1TB of leaked emails and documents released last month.

Baykara's attorney, Marc L. Mukasey, released a statement expressing shock at the death: "Mr. Baykara maintained his innocence and was looking forward to his day in court. His death is a tragic end to a complex case."

International implications

The Turkish Embassy in Washington has requested a full briefing on the investigation. Baykara, a dual citizen, had significant business interests in Turkey, where he was born.

"We expect a thorough and transparent investigation," said a Turkish diplomatic source who spoke on condition of anonymity.
