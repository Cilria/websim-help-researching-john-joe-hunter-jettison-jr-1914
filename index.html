Sen. Ossoff Launches 2028 Presidential Bid, First Major Democrat to Enter Race

December 19, 2026 - 10:30 AM EDT
By Rachel Anderson, CNN Senior Political Correspondent

ATLANTA (CNN) -- Senator Jon Ossoff of Georgia announced his candidacy for the 2028 Democratic presidential nomination Saturday, becoming the first major Democrat to officially enter what is expected to be a crowded primary field.

Ossoff, 41, made his announcement at a rally in Atlanta's Centennial Olympic Park, positioning himself as a voice for a new generation of leadership. "The time has come for fresh perspectives and bold solutions to build an America that works for everyone," Ossoff declared to a crowd of supporters.

The announcement comes as Vice President J.D. Vance is widely considered the frontrunner for the Republican nomination, following strong polling numbers and tacit support from President Donald Trump, who is constitutionally barred from seeking another term.

Democratic strategists anticipate a competitive primary season, with potential candidates including Pennsylvania Governor Josh Shapiro, Michigan Governor Gretchen Whitmer, California Governor Gavin Newsom, Illinois Governor JB Pritzker, and Maryland Governor Wes Moore all reportedly considering bids.

The Democratic party enters the race with momentum following recent midterm victories, having secured control of the House with 236 seats. However, Republicans maintain a narrow 51-49 majority in the Senate after flipping seats in North Carolina and Maine.

Sources close to DNC Chair Ken Martin reveal internal tensions regarding potential candidates. In a private conversation, Martin allegedly warned Governor Newsom against running, suggesting his candidacy could alienate crucial voter demographics that previously shifted away from the Democratic party.

The 2028 election represents a significant shift in American politics, marking the first presidential race since 2004 without Barack Obama, Joe Biden, George W. Bush, or Donald Trump on either party's ticket. Political analysts suggest this opens the door for new political dynamics and messaging.

"We're entering uncharted territory," said Dr. Jennifer Lawrence, professor of political science at Georgetown University. "The absence of these dominant political figures creates both opportunities and challenges for candidates seeking to define themselves in a post-Trump, post-Biden political landscape."

Ossoff's campaign emphasizes his record of bipartisan legislation and economic development initiatives in Georgia. His team has already assembled a national fundraising network, building on connections established during his high-profile Senate campaign.

Democratic party officials expect the field to expand rapidly in the coming months, with several potential candidates planning exploratory committees and listening tours in early primary states.
