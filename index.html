I've been reading about the Huns and I'm confused. Were the White Huns (Hephtalites) the same as the European Huns, or are we talking about completely different groups? The names are so similar but they seem to be from different time periods and locations.

CentralAsianHistory • 3h Central Asian History
They're completely different groups, despite the similar names! The European Huns (like Attila's people) were a nomadic confederation from the Central Asian steppes who appeared in Europe around 370 CE. The White Huns, also called the Hephtalites, were a different nomadic group who established an empire in Central Asia and parts of India around 450-560 CE. The name 'White Huns' was given by the Byzantines because they were lighter-skinned than the European Huns they were familiar with.

ByzantineHistoryPhd • 3h Byzantine History PhD
The Byzantines called them 'White Huns' to distinguish them from the European Huns they knew. The Hephtalites were actually a different ethnic group entirely. The European Huns were likely proto-Turkic peoples, while the Hephtalites (White Huns) were probably related to the Hua people who were of mixed Iranian and Turkic descent. The Byzantines used 'white' to indicate they were from the east - white being associated with the east in Byzantine symbolism.

SteppeArchaeologist • 2h
The confusion is understandable since both groups were steppe nomads who terrorized settled societies! The European Huns under Attila created a huge empire from the Rhine to the Urals, while the White Huns created a smaller but wealthy empire in Central Asia. The White Huns actually defeated the Sasanian Persians and even killed their emperor, but they never made it to Europe. The European Huns never made it to India. Different groups, different directions.

MedievalGenealogy • 2h
The naming confusion comes from Byzantine historians who used 'Hun' as a generic term for scary steppe nomads. The White Huns weren't even related to the European Huns! The European Huns were part of the same cultural group that later became the Magyars and Turks. The White Huns were more related to the Bactrians and Sogdians of Central Asia. It's like calling both the Vikings and the Mongols 'barbarians' - they're both scary foreign invaders, but completely different peoples.

AncientIranianScholar • 2h Iranian Studies
The White Huns were actually called the Hephthalites by the Persians, who had extensive contact with them. They were a major power in Central Asia and Afghanistan from about 450-560 CE. They were known for their distinctive elongated skulls (artificially shaped) and practice of polyandry (women having multiple husbands). The European Huns never practiced these customs. The Hephthalites were also known for their sophisticated administration and their ability to govern a multi-ethnic empire.
