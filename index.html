BREAKTHROUGH: Found James Monroe Crowder's complete Civil War service and discovered his shocking 1924 marriage to a 22-year-old!

UPDATE: After months of searching, I just cracked the James Monroe Crowder case! James Monroe Crowder (April 10, 1843 - July 20, 1929) was born in Crawford, MS to Edmund Crowder Sr. (1801-1865) and Martha Ann Wilks (1805-after 1870). He was their 8th child and never attended school - started working on the farm at age 8.

Here's the incredible discovery: I found his complete Civil War service records at the National Archives! His brothers Nathanial, David, Robert, and Edmund Jr. enlisted in November 1861. James joined in March 1862 at age 19 and served until December 1864, then reenlisted January 1865 until war's end. He was wounded in July 1864 - "shot through my shoulder and fell, breaking my left shoulder." His pension file confirms he was never captured by Union forces.

The heartbreaking part: Both Nathanial and David died during the war, leaving only Robert, Edmund Jr., and James as survivors. After the war, James moved east of Brooksville, MS where he worked as a farmer for the rest of his life. He was extremely poor and married Eliza (last name unknown) with whom he had 10 children.

The SHOCKING discovery: In 1924, at age 81, James married 22-year-old Susan (last name unknown)! They were married until his death in 1929. He spent his final 5 years living with his son Jefferson Carter Crowder in Brooksville.
