As a history professor specializing in 19th century rural America, I'm both laughing my ass off and wanting to bang my head against a wall. This Isaac Webster bullshit is a perfect example of how NOT to do historical research.

1. Always, ALWAYS check primary sources. Census records, land deeds, probate inventories. These are your bread and butter.

2. Be skeptical of family legends. They're often exaggerated or completely made up.

3. Context is key. Rural Illinois in the 1820s-50s was not a hotbed of philosophical discourse or epic adventures. It was mostly just people trying not to starve.

4. Absence of evidence is not evidence of absence, but it's also not an invitation to make shit up. If you can't find any records of someone doing something extraordinary, they probably didn't do anything extraordinary.

5. Remember, most people throughout history were not special. They were born, they worked, they had kids, they died. That's not boring, that's just life.

For fuck's sake, people, do better research or stick to writing historical fiction.
