What if the Mongol Empire never rose? How would world history change without Genghis Khan and the Mongol conquests?

I've been thinking about this a lot lately. The Mongol Empire reshaped the entire world - they connected East and West, destroyed civilizations, created new trade routes, and spread technology. But what if Genghis Khan was never born, or if the Mongol tribes never united? How different would our world be today?

Some points to consider: - The Silk Road might have remained fragmented - Russia might never have become a unified state - China could have remained divided for centuries longer - Islamic civilization might have continued its golden age - Europe might not have had the Renaissance when it did

ByzantineExpert • 3 hours ago Professional Historian
Professional medieval historian here. Without the Mongols, the Islamic Golden Age would have continued for at least another century. Baghdad would have remained the world's intellectual capital. The House of Wisdom wouldn't have been destroyed, and Islamic mathematicians and astronomers would have made even more breakthroughs.

Europe would have remained a backwater for much longer. The Renaissance relied heavily on Islamic texts that were preserved and transmitted through the Mongol trade networks. Without that, Europe might not have industrialized until the 18th or 19th century.
