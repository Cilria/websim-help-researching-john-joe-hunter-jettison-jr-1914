Francis brothers, including GOP state senator, charged in $9B theft scheme after judge brother recusal

By NBC News Staff
Updated 6:23 PM ET, Tue July 11, 2023
Republican State Senator Carl William Francis (center) and brothers James (left) and David Anderholt Francis Jr. (right) leave federal court in Oxford after new charges were filed Tuesday.

Oxford, Miss. (NBC News) — Federal prosecutors unsealed sweeping new corruption charges Tuesday against three brothers, including a sitting Republican state senator, in what authorities describe as a $9 billion embezzlement and money-laundering conspiracy that spanned six years and involved a previously undisclosed conflict of interest involving a state judge.

State Sen. Carl William Francis, 52, his brother James Francis, 49, and their younger brother David Anderholt Francis Jr., 45 — who served as the judge in their original 2018 trial — were all arrested Tuesday morning and charged with conspiracy to commit wire fraud, money laundering, and obstruction of justice.

The new indictment alleges the brothers orchestrated an elaborate scheme to siphon public funds earmarked for infrastructure projects, transferring the money through shell companies to the Cayman Islands between 2017 and 2023. The total amount stolen — $9.1 billion — makes this one of the largest public-corruption cases in U.S. history.

“These three brothers treated the state treasury like their personal piggy bank,” said U.S. Attorney Jennifer McAllister at a press conference. “What makes this case particularly egregious is that one brother was sitting in judgment of the other two while all three were actively participating in the same criminal enterprise.”

The 2018 trial

David Anderholt Francis Jr. was assigned to preside over the original trial of Carl and James in 2018 on related fraud charges. At the time, prosecutors say, all three brothers were already deeply involved in the conspiracy. David never disclosed his relationship to the defendants and dismissed the charges on procedural grounds, allowing the scheme to continue for five more years.

“Judge Francis used his position to shield his brothers from accountability,” said FBI Special Agent in Charge Laura Kim. “That abuse of judicial power allowed the conspiracy to metastasize into the multibillion-dollar fraud we see today.”

According to the indictment, the brothers used encrypted messaging apps to coordinate their efforts. In one 2019 exchange, Carl allegedly wrote to David: “Keep the wolves at bay, little brother. The river of gold flows to the islands.” David responded with a thumbs-up emoji.

How the scheme worked

Prosecutors say Carl, as chair of the Senate Appropriations Committee, steered lucrative state contracts to shell companies secretly controlled by James. Those companies would then submit inflated invoices and kick back the excess to offshore accounts held by all three brothers.

The money allegedly funded lavish lifestyles: private jets, a 14-bedroom beach compound in Grand Cayman, a $45 million art collection, and a 200-foot yacht named “Triple F” (Francis Family Foundation).

“They weren’t subtle,” said Mississippi State Auditor Sarah Lee. “One month Carl would approve a $500 million highway contract, and the next month James would buy another mansion in the Caymans.”

Finally caught

The scheme unraveled in March when investigators with the Cayman Islands Monetary Authority flagged suspicious transfers totaling $2.3 billion from Mississippi state accounts to a single offshore entity. That tip led to a six-month federal probe involving forensic accountants and international cooperation.

All three brothers were taken into custody without incident at their homes in Oxford. Each faces up to 50 years in prison if convicted on all counts. They are expected to make initial appearances in U.S. District Court later this week.

“This case should serve as a warning to anyone who thinks family ties and political power can shield them from justice,” said Attorney General Dana Remini. “The FBI and our partners will follow the money wherever it leads — even to the bench.”

Carl Francis has served in the Mississippi Senate since 2012 and was considered a potential candidate for governor in 2027. James operated a consulting firm that did business with multiple state agencies. David was appointed to the bench in 2016 by then-Gov. Phil Bryant.

The Mississippi Senate has scheduled an emergency session for Thursday to discuss expulsion proceedings against Sen. Francis. Under state law, a two-thirds vote would be required to remove him from office.

Gov. Tate Reeves called the allegations “a betrayal of the public trust” and said he would appoint a special prosecutor to investigate whether any other state officials were involved.

The brothers are being held without bond, deemed flight risks due to their extensive overseas holdings. Their attorneys did not immediately respond to requests for comment.
