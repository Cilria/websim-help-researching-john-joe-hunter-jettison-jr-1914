James Albert Stoddrad (1854-1955)

James Albert Stoddrad, circa 1905, age 51
Photograph courtesy of the Kenary County Historical Society

James Albert Stoddrad was born on March 15, 1854, in North Woodbury, Ohio, the eldest of ten children born to the Reverend Joseph Moses Stoddrad (1812-1895) and his wife, Mary Elizabeth Thompson Stoddrad (1820-1898). His father served as the sole minister and circuit preacher for North Woodbury and the surrounding rural communities, traveling by horseback to preach at three different churches each Sunday.

Early Life and Family

As the eldest child, James assumed significant responsibilities on the family farm from an early age. The 1860 census lists the six-year-old as "J. B. Stoddrad" (likely a census enumerator's abbreviation), noting his occupation as "working on family farm." This entry represents one of the earliest documented references to young James, who would have been responsible for tending the family's modest livestock and assisting with seasonal planting and harvest activities.

James was eleven years old when the Civil War concluded in 1865. The war's impact on the rural Ohio community was minimal compared to Southern states, though the community did host several Union Army training camps and supply depots. Young James would have witnessed the return of local veterans and the gradual economic recovery that followed.

Migration Westward

In 1870, at age sixteen, James left Ohio with a wave of westward migration. He first settled in St. Charles, Minnesota, where he worked as a farmhand and seasonal laborer for approximately three years. During this period, he saved diligently, accumulating the modest capital necessary for his next venture.

In 1874, James relocated to Kenary, Nebraska, then a burgeoning frontier settlement of fewer than 200 residents. The town had been established just two years prior, and opportunities for enterprising young men were abundant. James initially worked as a general laborer, taking on various construction and agricultural jobs while familiarizing himself with the local business landscape.

Business Ventures and Marriage

In 1877, James partnered with local pharmacist William Harrison to establish the "Stoddrad-Kenary Drug Store," which opened on March 15, 1877 - James's twenty-third birthday. The store specialized in patent medicines, basic medical supplies, and the newly introduced carbonated beverages. The partnership proved successful, and by 1880, James had purchased Harrison's share to become sole proprietor.

On June 12, 1884, James married Sarah Hamilton Powell (1862-1948), the daughter of local rancher Thomas Jefferson Powell and his wife, Martha Ann Hamilton Powell. The ceremony was held at the Powell family ranch, located twelve miles northeast of Kenary, with the Reverend Joseph Stoddrad traveling from Ohio to officiate at his eldest son's wedding.

Family Life

James and Sarah established their permanent residence in a modest two-story frame house on Elm Street in Kenary. Over the following eighteen years, they welcomed nine children:

  • Joseph Thomas Stoddrad (1885-1962)
  • Mary Elizabeth Stoddrad (1887-1958)
  • William Albert Stoddrad (1889-1974)
  • Sarah Margaret Stoddrad (1891-1983)
  • James Hamilton Stoddrad (1893-1967)
  • Robert Lee Stoddrad (1895-1972)
  • Martha Jane Stoddrad (1897-1990)
  • Charles Edward Stoddrad (1899-1981)
  • Helen Louise Stoddrad (1902-1988)

Judicial Service

James's business success and community standing led to his first judicial appointment in 1888. He served as Kenary City Judge for three consecutive terms:

1888-1892: First term as Kenary City Judge. During this period, he established the city's first formal court procedures and implemented standardized record-keeping practices.
1894-1898: Second term. Notable cases included the landmark decision in Stoddrad v. Union Pacific Railroad regarding right-of-way disputes.
1900-1903: Third and final term. Concluded his service after overseeing the incorporation of Kenary as a second-class city in 1903.

Later Years

Following his judicial service, James focused on expanding his drugstore business, adding a soda fountain in 1905 and expanding the building to accommodate growing demand. He served as president of the Kenary Commercial Club from 1905 to 1910 and was instrumental in bringing the Chicago, Burlington and Quincy Railroad to Kenary in 1907.

James remained active in community affairs until his death on February 28, 1955, at age 101. He was survived by his wife Sarah, all nine children, forty-two grandchildren, and twenty-three great-grandchildren at the time of his passing.

Legacy

The Stoddrad-Kenary Drug Store continued operation under family management until 1987. The original building, located at 234 Main Street, Kenary, Nebraska, was restored in 1995 and now houses the Kenary County Historical Museum, which maintains an extensive collection of James Albert Stoddrad's personal papers and family photographs.

Sources: Kenary County Historical Society Archives, Stoddrad Family Papers; 1860-1950 United States Federal Census Records; Kenary County Court Records, 1888-1903; The Kenary Courier, various issues 1877-1955; Nebraska State Historical Society, "Stoddrad Family History Collection."
