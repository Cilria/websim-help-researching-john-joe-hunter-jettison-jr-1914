Armed Chinese and Russian militias clash at border as both nations declare territorial expansion

By CNN International Correspondent
November 11, 2026

Beijing (CNN) — Armed Chinese citizens have begun crossing the border into Russian territory, seizing control of small villages while Russian troops opened fire on the advancing militias, as both nations declared territorial expansion and moved closer to full-scale war.

The dramatic escalation began early Tuesday morning when Chinese militia groups, apparently organized with government backing, crossed the border in multiple locations. Russian border guards responded with live fire, triggering what appears to be the opening phase of a territorial conflict that threatens to engulf the entire region.

[Image: Chinese militias crossing border]
Chinese militia groups have crossed the border, seizing Russian villages

Simultaneously, Russian ultranationalist militias have been observed crossing into Chinese territory, burning villages and engaging in firefights with Chinese border forces. The coordinated nature of these incursions suggests both governments are actively supporting proxy forces rather than attempting to prevent escalation.

"Russia is a terrorist scum state. Only extermination is left."

Russian President Vladimir Putin addressed the nation Tuesday evening, declaring: "China is a terrorist scum state, only extermination is left. The Chinese barbarians have shown their true nature as backstabbing savages who must be completely destroyed."

Chinese Foreign Minister Wang Yi responded in kind, stating: "Russia is a barbarian state of thugs, criminals and monsters. It should be destroyed to the ashbins of history. The Russian dogs have violated every principle of civilized behavior and must be eliminated from the face of the earth."

The rhetoric has escalated dramatically as both sides accuse each other of funding separatist movements. Russia claims China has been secretly financing separatist groups in Siberia and the Russian Far East, providing weapons and training to ethnic minorities seeking independence from Moscow.

"We have concrete evidence that Chinese intelligence agencies have been funding and arming separatist movements across Russia," claimed Russian Defense Minister Sergei Shoigu. "They seek to break apart our nation, to destroy the Russian state from within. This is an act of war that demands total military response."

[Image: Russian militias burning Chinese village]
Russian ultranationalist militias have burned Chinese border villages

China has leveled similar accusations, claiming that Russia has been secretly supporting separatist movements in Xinjiang, Tibet, and Inner Mongolia. Chinese state media has broadcast what it claims is evidence of Russian intelligence officers meeting with separatist leaders.

"The Russian barbarians have been plotting to break apart China for years," declared Chinese Defense Minister Wei Fenghe. "They fund terrorists, they arm separatists, they seek to destroy our great nation from within. But China will not be broken. We will destroy Russia completely."

The territorial declarations mark a dangerous new phase in the conflict. Both nations have officially declared their intention to expand their territory at the expense of the other, with Chinese officials claiming historical rights to vast portions of the Russian Far East, while Russian officials claim Chinese territory along the Amur River.

"Every Chinese citizen must be ready to take up arms. Every Russian must be destroyed."

Chinese state media has begun broadcasting detailed maps showing "historical Chinese territory" that encompasses much of Siberia, including the cities of Vladivostok, Khabarovsk, and even parts of Lake Baikal. Commentators speak of "reclaiming what was stolen from China" and "restoring the natural borders of the Middle Kingdom."

Russian television has responded with maps showing "Greater Russia" that includes large portions of northern China, particularly Manchuria. State media commentators speak of "liberating Chinese territory from the communist yoke" and "restoring Russian sovereignty over lands historically part of the Russian Empire."

The militia activity appears to be coordinated with these territorial claims. Chinese militias operating in Russian territory have been documented raising Chinese flags over captured villages and declaring them "liberated territory of the motherland." Similar scenes have been reported in Chinese villages seized by Russian militias.

"We are witnessing the beginning of a territorial war that could reshape the Asian continent," warns Dr. Alexander Lukin, director of the Russian Academy of Sciences' Institute of Far Eastern Studies. "Both sides have abandoned diplomacy and are now actively pursuing territorial expansion. This is no longer about protests or embassy attacks - this is the opening phase of a major war."

[Image: Chinese territorial claims map]
Chinese state media broadcasts maps showing territorial claims in Russia

Military forces on both sides have reportedly been given orders to "secure territory" rather than simply defend the border. Chinese troops have been observed establishing forward positions inside what was previously undisputed Russian territory, while Russian forces have done the same in Chinese territory.

"Every Chinese citizen must be ready to take up arms," declared one Chinese military official during a televised briefing. "Every Russian must be destroyed. We will not stop until the Russian state is completely eliminated and Chinese sovereignty is restored over all historical territory."

The international community has responded with alarm to these developments. The United Nations Security Council held an emergency session Tuesday night, though both China and Russia vetoed any resolution condemning the territorial aggression.

"We are looking at the potential for the largest territorial conflict since World War II," warned one Western diplomat. "Both nations have now committed themselves to expansion rather than negotiation. The question is no longer whether war will break out, but how large it will become."

[Image: Military buildup at border]
Both nations have deployed military forces to support territorial claims

Civilian populations on both sides of the border have begun evacuating as the militia activity escalates. Chinese citizens in border regions report being armed by local authorities and encouraged to "reclaim stolen territory," while Russian citizens describe similar calls to "defend the motherland from Chinese aggression."

"This is no longer a diplomatic crisis or even a limited military conflict," said one international relations expert. "Both nations have now declared their intention to expand their territory at the expense of the other. They've abandoned any pretense of peaceful coexistence. We're watching the opening moves of what could become a major continental war."
