China declares war on Russia, vows to "destroy the nation once and for all"

By CNN International Correspondent
November 14, 2026

Beijing (CNN) — China has officially declared war on Russia, with President Li Qiang announcing that "the hour of justice has come" and vowing to "destroy Russia once and for all as a nation," marking the most significant military escalation between major powers since World War II.

The formal declaration of war came during an emergency session of the National People's Congress early Friday morning, where Chinese leaders unanimously approved military action against what they termed "the savage Russian monsters who have plagued humanity for too long."

[Image: Chinese President announcing war]
President Li Qiang announces China's declaration of war against Russia

"The Russian barbarians have finally forced our hand," President Li declared to thunderous applause from assembled delegates. "For too long, we have tolerated their existence, their insults, their crimes against civilization. But no more. The hour of justice has come. We will destroy Russia completely and ensure these monsters never threaten humanity again."

"The hour of justice has come. We will destroy Russia once and for all as a nation."

Russian President Vladimir Putin responded within hours, addressing the Russian people in a televised broadcast where he denounced the Chinese declaration as "the final proof that the Chinese scum have lost all connection to civilized behavior."

"The Chinese dogs have finally shown their true nature," Putin declared, his voice dripping with contempt. "They speak of justice while planning genocide. They speak of civilization while acting like the most primitive savages. Russia will not be intimidated by these yellow monsters. We will destroy them completely."

The Chinese declaration comes after weeks of escalating border clashes, embassy attacks, and increasingly violent rhetoric between the two nuclear-armed powers. The formal declaration of war represents the culmination of tensions that have been building since late October, when Chinese social media users began openly mocking Russians and claiming historical rights to Russian territory.

Within minutes of the war declaration, Chinese state media began broadcasting detailed military plans, showing animated maps of proposed invasion routes into Siberia and the Russian Far East. Commentators spoke confidently of a "72-hour victory" and the "complete elimination of the Russian state."

[Image: Chinese military mobilization]
Chinese military forces mobilize along the Russian border

"The Russian barbarians have signed their own death warrant," declared Chinese Defense Minister Wei Fenghe during a military briefing. "We have assembled the largest invasion force in human history. Over 3 million troops, 15,000 tanks, 8,000 aircraft, all ready to sweep across the border and erase the Russian menace from existence."

Chinese military officials outlined a three-phase plan for the complete destruction of Russia. Phase One calls for the immediate seizure of all Russian territory east of the Ural Mountains, including the cities of Vladivostok, Khabarovsk, and Irkutsk. Phase Two involves the capture of Siberia and the establishment of "liberated zones" for ethnic minorities. Phase Three envisions the complete collapse of the Russian government and the division of remaining Russian territory among neighboring countries.

"We will not stop until the last Russian city has fallen," Wei declared. "We will not rest until the Russian flag has been completely erased from the map. This is not conquest - this is cleansing. The world must be purified of the Russian disease."

"We will not stop until the Russian flag has been completely erased from the map."

Russian officials responded with equally apocalyptic rhetoric. Defense Minister Sergei Shoigu announced that Russia had placed its entire nuclear arsenal on high alert and was prepared to use "every weapon in our arsenal" to defend against the Chinese invasion.

"The Chinese scum have made the fatal mistake of underestimating Russian resolve," Shoigu declared. "We have 6,000 nuclear warheads ready to turn China into a radioactive wasteland. If they want war, we will give them a war that ends with the complete extermination of the Chinese race."

International reaction to the war declaration has been swift and alarmed. The United Nations Security Council held an emergency session within hours, though both China and Russia vetoed any resolution calling for restraint. NATO announced it was placing all forces on high alert, while neighboring countries began emergency evacuations of border regions.

"We are witnessing the beginning of what could become the most devastating conflict in human history," warned UN Secretary-General António Guterres. "Two nuclear-armed powers have now committed themselves to the complete destruction of the other. The consequences for humanity could be catastrophic."

Chinese state media has begun broadcasting instructions to citizens on how to prepare for potential nuclear war, while Russian television has aired detailed programs about Chinese "war crimes" and the need for "total extermination of the Chinese threat."

[Image: Nuclear missile launchers]
Both nations have placed nuclear forces on high alert

"Every Chinese citizen must be prepared to make whatever sacrifices are necessary to ensure the final destruction of Russia," declared one Chinese state television commentator. "This is not merely a war between nations - this is a war between civilization and barbarism, between humanity and monsters."

Russian media has responded with equally extreme rhetoric, with commentators calling for the "complete sterilization of Chinese territory" and the "eradication of every last Chinese from the face of the earth."

"The Chinese have shown themselves to be subhuman," declared one Russian television host. "They are not people - they are a disease that must be completely eliminated. Nuclear weapons are too good for them. They deserve to be exterminated like the vermin they are."

Military analysts warn that the conflict could escalate rapidly beyond conventional warfare. Both nations possess massive nuclear arsenals, and the extreme rhetoric being used by both sides suggests that nuclear weapons use is being seriously considered.

[Image: Evacuation of border regions]
Neighboring countries evacuate border regions as war escalates

"We are potentially looking at the first nuclear war in human history," warned one Western military analyst. "Both sides have now committed themselves not just to military victory, but to the complete extermination of the other. When you have two nuclear powers using this kind of language, the risk of nuclear escalation is extremely high."

Civilian populations on both sides of the border have begun mass evacuations as military forces mobilize for what both sides are calling the "final battle." Chinese citizens have been instructed to prepare for potential nuclear attack, while Russian citizens have been told to expect "total war" that could involve weapons of mass destruction.

"This is no longer about territory or politics," said one international relations expert. "This has become a war of extermination. Both sides have publicly committed themselves to the complete destruction of the other. We're looking at the potential end of both nations as functioning states."
