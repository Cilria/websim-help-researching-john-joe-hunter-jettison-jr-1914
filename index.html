LIVE UPDATES: Peru invades Bolivia after alleged border attack

By CNN Staff
⚠️ This is a developing story. Updates will be posted as they become available.
[MAP: Peruvian forces advancing into Bolivian territory]
11:43 PM ET

Peruvian forces capture Bolivian city of Desaguadero
Peruvian state media reports that regime forces have taken control of Desaguadero, a key Bolivian border town. Video shows Humala's forces raising the Incan flag over the city center.

11:15 PM ET

Reports of advancing columns toward La Paz
Multiple sources confirm that three Peruvian army columns, totaling approximately 200,000 troops, are advancing toward La Paz. Bolivian military appears to be in disorganized retreat.

10:47 PM ET

Humala declares "the fake nation of Bolivia will be erased"
In a televised address from the presidential palace, Humala stated: "The terrorist attack this morning leaves us no choice. The scummery of the fake nation of Bolivia will be removed from history. The restoration begins now."

10:23 PM ET

Border gate destroyed by Peruvian forces
Eyewitnesses report that Peruvian military engineers used explosives to destroy the main border crossing between Peru and Bolivia at the Kasani-DESAGUADERO crossing point.

9:15 PM ET

UN Security Council emergency session called
Chilean delegation requests immediate UN Security Council meeting. Bolivian ambassador to the UN claims "unprovoked aggression" and requests international intervention.

8:30 PM ET

Peru launches invasion of Bolivia
Peruvian military forces cross the border en masse following President Humala's declaration of war. Reports indicate upwards of 200,000 troops have been mobilized through the mandatory draft implemented last year.

6:45 PM ET

Humala claims terrorist attack originated from Bolivia
President Humala addresses the nation, alleging that a terrorist attack that killed 12 Peruvian soldiers was launched from Bolivian territory. Independent verification of the attack has not been confirmed.
