Newsom Rules Out 2028 Bid as Whitmer Prepares Campaign Launch

Published 9:32 AM EST, January 15, 2027
By Jeff Zeleny and Dana Bash, CNN

California Governor Gavin Newsom announced today he will not seek the Democratic presidential nomination in 2028, while Michigan Governor Gretchen Whitmer is preparing to launch her campaign, according to multiple sources familiar with their plans.

Newsom's decision, announced at a press conference in Sacramento, comes as he prepares to leave office on January 20, when Vice President Kamala Harris will be sworn in as California's next governor, following her recent resignation and appointment.

"While I deeply believe in the future of our party and our nation, this is not my moment," Newsom said. "The Democratic Party has an incredible slate of leaders ready to take the helm, and I look forward to supporting our nominee."

Meanwhile, Whitmer's team has begun staffing up for what sources describe as an "imminent campaign launch." The Michigan governor, who won reelection in 2022 by a substantial margin, has been quietly building a national network of donors and supporters.

The Democratic field has begun taking shape with Senator Jon Ossoff of Georgia and Maryland Governor Wes Moore already declaring their candidacies. Pennsylvania Governor Josh Shapiro and Illinois Governor J.B. Pritzker remain potential contenders.

In a surprising development on the Republican side, Donald Trump Jr. announced his own presidential bid, directly challenging Vice President J.D. Vance, whom he had previously endorsed. The move signals a significant rupture in the Trump political alliance that has dominated Republican politics.

"The America First agenda needs new blood and bold leadership," Trump Jr. declared in a video announcement. "While I respect the Vice President, I believe I'm better positioned to carry forward my father's legacy."

Democratic strategists suggest Whitmer's entry could reshape the primary landscape. "Governor Whitmer brings proven electoral success in a crucial Midwest battleground state," said Democratic strategist David Axelrod. "Her record on economic development and infrastructure could appeal to both progressive and moderate voters."

Sources close to Whitmer indicate she plans to focus her campaign on economic mobility, manufacturing revival, and protecting democratic institutions. Her formal announcement is expected within the next two weeks.
