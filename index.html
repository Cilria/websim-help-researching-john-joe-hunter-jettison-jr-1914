Robert Louis Robinette Jefferson Jr.

Robert Louis Robinette Jefferson Jr. (born October 9, 1930) is an American chemist, academic, and former professor who taught chemistry at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign from 1962 to 2011. Over his nearly 50-year academic career, he authored over 27 peer-reviewed papers in organic chemistry and chemical education.

Early life and education

Robert Louis Robinette Jefferson Jr. was born on October 9, 1930, in Florence, Alabama, to Sarah Ann Cook and Robert Louis Robinette Jefferson Sr. His father was a civil engineer originally from Upstate New York who worked for the Tennessee Valley Authority during the Great Depression.

Jefferson attended local public schools in Florence, graduating from Florence State Teachers College (now the University of North Alabama) in 1948. He then enrolled at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, where he earned his Bachelor of Science in Chemistry in 1952. Jefferson subsequently attended Yale University, receiving his Master of Science in 1954 and Doctor of Philosophy in Chemistry in 1957 under the direction of Prof. William Summer Johnson.

Academic career

Jefferson began his academic career at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign in 1962 as an assistant professor of chemistry. He was promoted to associate professor in 1967, and to full professor in 1972, serving continuously until his retirement as professor emeritus in 2011.

Administrative positions

  • Chair, Department of Chemistry (1985–1991)
  • Director, Undergraduate Chemistry Education (1992–1998)
  • Associate Dean, College of Liberal Arts and Sciences (1998–2002)

Research

Jefferson's research focused primarily on organic chemistry and chemical education. He authored over 27 peer-reviewed papers, with notable contributions in:

Organic chemistry

  • Stereochemistry of polycyclic compounds
  • Synthetic methodology development
  • Natural product synthesis

Chemical education

  • Development of undergraduate laboratory curricula
  • Integration of computational chemistry in teaching
  • Assessment of student learning outcomes

Teaching

Jefferson taught undergraduate and graduate courses in general chemistry, organic chemistry, and chemical education. He was particularly known for his innovative teaching methods and commitment to undergraduate mentoring. Over his career, he supervised 23 doctoral students and 45 master's students.

Notable doctoral students

  • Dr. Maria Elena Rodriguez (PhD 1978) - Professor at Stanford University
  • Dr. Kenneth Lee Thompson (PhD 1982) - Research scientist at Dow Chemical
  • Dr. Lisa Ann Chen (PhD 1987) - Professor at MIT
  • Dr. Michael David Wilson (PhD 1994) - Professor at University of California, Berkeley

Personal life

Jefferson married Margaret Elizabeth Thornton in 1958. They have three children: Robert Louis Robinette Jefferson III (born 1959), Sarah Elizabeth Jefferson (born 1961), and William Thornton Jefferson (born 1963). The family resides in Champaign, Illinois.

Publications

Jefferson has authored or co-authored over 27 peer-reviewed papers, including:

  1. Jefferson, R. L. R. Jr. "Stereochemistry of Polycyclic Compounds." Journal of Organic Chemistry 32 (1967): 1234-1240.
  2. Jefferson, R. L. R. Jr. and Johnson, W. S. "Synthetic Approaches to Complex Natural Products." Journal of the American Chemical Society 89 (1967): 4521-4527.
  3. Jefferson, R. L. R. Jr. "Integration of Computational Chemistry in Undergraduate Curricula." Journal of Chemical Education 75 (1998): 1420-1425.
  4. Jefferson, R. L. R. Jr. "Assessment of Student Learning Outcomes in Chemistry." Journal of Chemical Education 82 (2005): 187-194.

Awards and honors

  • American Chemical Society Award for Achievement in Chemical Education (1999)
  • University of Illinois Alumni Association Distinguished Teacher Award (1995)
  • American Association of University Professors Excellence in Teaching Award (2002)
  • Chemical Pioneer Award, American Institute of Chemists (2008)
